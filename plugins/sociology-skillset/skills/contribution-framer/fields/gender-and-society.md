# Field Profile: Gender & Society (Contribution Framer)

This field profile adjusts contribution-framer guidance for articles targeting gender studies venues. Based on genre analysis of 39 research articles from *Gender & Society* (2024–2026), compared to the flagship corpus (n=115, *AJS*, *ASR*, *Social Problems*, *Social Forces*).

## When to Apply This Profile

Apply when the target venue or subfield is feminist sociology or gender studies. Common venues:

- *Gender & Society*
- *Signs: Journal of Women in Culture and Society*
- *Feminist Studies*
- *Journal of Marriage and Family* (gender-focused)
- Gender-focused special issues in general journals

Also apply when the article's core topic involves gender inequality, sexuality, masculinity/femininity, reproductive politics, or intersectional analysis of gender — even if targeting a generalist journal — as G&S conventions shape reader expectations.

## How G&S Differs from Generalist Sociology

7 key findings from comparing G&S contribution framing (n=39) to the flagship corpus (n=115, *AJS*, *ASR*, *Social Problems*, *Social Forces*):

| Finding | Flagships | G&S | Implication |
|---------|-----------|-----|-------------|
| Concept-Building dominance | ~27% | **62%** | CB is the modal type by a wide margin; G&S rewards conceptual innovation |
| Process-Tracing is rare | ~27% | **8%** | PT is unusual; mechanism-mapping is not the journal's orientation |
| Gap-Filler is nearly absent | ~9% | **3%** | Pure empirical gap-filling without conceptual contribution is very rare |
| Factor-Identifying is rare | ~24% | **8%** | Adjudicating competing explanations is uncommon; only quantitative articles |
| CB concepts are domain-specific | High-abstraction | **All mid-range** | Concepts stay close to their empirical domain; don't push for excessive abstraction |
| Threading is strong | Strong (90%+) | **Strong (92%)** | G&S articles thread key terms tightly across all sections |
| Feminist theory is the default anchor | Various traditions | **Feminist theory universal** | Every article anchors in feminist theory; intersectionality is the organizational logic |

**Root pattern**: G&S is a concept-building journal. Nearly two-thirds of articles introduce or develop new concepts, and all of them are mid-range and domain-specific rather than aspiring to general social theory. Theory-Extension is the secondary mode (21%), while Process-Tracing, Factor-Identifying, and Gap-Filler are rare. Threading is strong — key terms saturate all sections.

## Decision Tree Adjustments for G&S

The same 5-question decision tree applies, but calibration shifts substantially:

### 1. Concept-Building Is the Expected Mode

At G&S, CB is not just one option among five — it's the dominant contribution type (62%). If a user's article develops a new concept or typology, CB framing is the natural choice. Don't push toward PT or FI reframing unless the article's logic clearly demands it.

### 2. Gap-Filler Is a Warning Sign

Unlike social movements venues where GF is rehabilitated, **at G&S, gap-filling alone is insufficient**. Only 1 of 39 articles was coded as pure GF. If a user's article fills an empirical gap, help them identify the conceptual contribution that makes it more than gap-filling. Reframing as CB (naming what the gap reveals) or TE (extending a framework to the new domain) is almost always necessary.

### 3. Process-Tracing Is Unusual

At flagships (especially AJS), PT is a dominant mode. **At G&S, PT is rare (8%).** If a user's article naturally traces a causal mechanism, it may be better positioned at a flagship. If targeting G&S, consider whether the mechanism can be named as a concept (reframe as CB) or whether it extends an existing framework (reframe as TE).

### 4. Concept-Building Should Be Mid-Range

All CB articles in the G&S corpus develop mid-range, domain-specific concepts — none aspire to high-abstraction general social theory. Concepts like "carceral care economy," "spatialized feelings," "feminist erosion," "diasporic capital," "refusing gender," and "lenses of impossibility" are tightly bound to their empirical domains. Don't push users toward excessive abstraction.

### 5. Theory-Extension Leans Feminist

TE articles at G&S extend feminist theoretical frameworks — doing gender, intersectionality, controlling images, intimate labor, compulsory heterosexuality — to new populations, contexts, or domains. The frameworks being extended are almost always feminist in origin, not general sociological theory.

## Threading Adjustments

### Threading Strength

G&S articles thread key terms strongly (92% strong threading). This is comparable to flagship journals and notably stronger than social movements venues. Key concept terms saturate every section from abstract through conclusion.

**Recommendation**: Generate full threading vocabulary and enforce it. G&S expects tight conceptual coherence across all sections.

### Threading by Type at G&S

| Type | Typical Threading Strength | Notes |
|------|---------------------------|-------|
| CB | **Strong** | Coined concept name threads through every section; this is the signature G&S pattern |
| TE | **Strong** | Extended framework name + new application domain thread consistently |
| FI | **Moderate to Strong** | Competing explanations and key variables thread through; less conceptually distinctive |
| PT | **Strong** | Mechanism vocabulary threads, but rare at G&S |
| GF | **N/A** | Too rare to characterize |

## Signature Phrases by Type at G&S

### CB at G&S

- "We introduce the concept of [X] to capture..."
- "We develop the concept of [X] to describe..."
- "[Coined term] refers to..."
- "We propose [X] as an analytical framework for..."
- Typology language: "three forms of," "four dimensions of," "a continuum from X to Y"
- "This concept builds on [existing framework] by adding..."

### TE at G&S

- "We extend [framework] to [new domain/population]..."
- "Applying [theorist]'s framework to [context] reveals..."
- "By centering [population], we show that [framework] operates differently..."
- "This extends [concept] beyond [original scope] to..."

### FI at G&S

- "We adjudicate between [explanation A] and [explanation B]..."
- "Our results support [X] over [Y]..."
- "Contrary to the [dominant explanation], we find..."

## Field-Specific Prohibited Moves

In addition to the general prohibited moves in SKILL.md:

### Don't Do These When Targeting G&S

- **Don't frame GF as sufficient** — Pure gap-filling is nearly absent at G&S; every article needs a conceptual contribution
- **Don't expect AJS-style mechanism development** — PT is rare; extended mechanism elaboration reads as out of genre
- **Don't push for high-abstraction concepts** — Mid-range, domain-specific concepts are the norm and valued
- **Don't ignore feminist theoretical grounding** — Every article at G&S anchors in feminist theory; a contribution that doesn't engage feminist scholarship signals unfamiliarity with the venue
- **Don't treat intersectionality as optional** — Intersectionality is the organizational logic of G&S, not one framework among many
- **Don't position quantitative adjudication as the primary mode** — FI is rare (8%); if your article tests competing explanations, the conceptual framing still needs to be foregrounded

## Writing Checklists

### Before Finalizing a G&S Contribution Profile

Verify:

- [ ] **Type is appropriate**: CB is the default at G&S (62%). If not CB, verify the alternative type is justified
- [ ] **Conceptual contribution is clear**: Even TE and FI articles at G&S have a conceptual payoff — the contribution is never purely empirical
- [ ] **Concept scope is mid-range**: If CB, the concept should be domain-specific, not aspiring to general social theory
- [ ] **Feminist theory grounds the contribution**: The theoretical anchor should include feminist scholarship, not just general sociology
- [ ] **Intersectionality is integrated**: The contribution should address how the concept/extension operates across intersecting axes of inequality
- [ ] **Threading is strong**: Key terms should saturate every section — abstract, introduction, theory, methods, findings, conclusion
- [ ] **Gap-Filler is reframed**: If the initial framing is GF, help reframe as CB or TE — pure gap-filling is insufficient for G&S
- [ ] **Non-standard types flagged**: If the article is primarily methodological or a review piece, note that the 5-type typology may not apply
