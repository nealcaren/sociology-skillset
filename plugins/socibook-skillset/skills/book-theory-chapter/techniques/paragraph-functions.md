# Paragraph Functions in Theory Chapters

This guide identifies ten paragraph-level functions that recur across theory chapters in political sociology and American politics monographs. Each function has a defined purpose, a characteristic internal structure, and real examples drawn from the corpus.

Theory chapters occupy a unique position in the book: they must translate an original intellectual framework into portable claims the reader can carry through the empirical chapters that follow. Where introductions sell the puzzle and empirical chapters deliver the evidence, theory chapters build the machinery. The paragraph functions here reflect that machinery-building purpose.

Use this guide in two ways: (1) when drafting, to decide what each paragraph needs to accomplish; (2) when revising, to diagnose paragraphs that are doing too much, too little, or the wrong work for their position.

---

## The Five Theory-Chapter Clusters

Before the paragraph functions, a note on the five clusters of theory chapters observed in the corpus. Cluster membership shapes which paragraph functions dominate and in what order they appear.

| Cluster | Logic | Exemplars |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| **Deductive-Formal** | Builds from assumptions to hypotheses via a causal model; numbered predictions; emphasis on scope conditions | Bolton & Thrower Ch. 2 |
| **Interpretive-Conceptual** | Develops a new vocabulary and conceptual framework through extended contrast with existing approaches; few or no formal hypotheses | Lichterman Ch. 1; Smith & King Ch. 2 |
| **Historical-Institutionalist** | Traces causal mechanisms through institutional dynamics; typologies and feedback loops rather than formal hypotheses | Patashnik Ch. 2 |
| **Behavioral-Spillover** | Specifies the psychological or attitudinal mechanism through which an independent variable produces political effects; conditions ripe for the mechanism | Tesler Chs. 1-2 |
| **Realist-Strategic** | Redefines familiar concepts (e.g., presidential power) by adding a neglected dimension; derives observable implications from the redefinition | Lowande Ch. 2 |

## The Ten Paragraph Functions

### 1. CONCEPT_DEFINE

**Purpose:** Introduce and define a key term that the book will use as a load-bearing concept. The definition paragraph establishes what the term means, distinguishes it from adjacent concepts, and gives the reader a handle to carry forward.

**Typical position:** Early in the chapter, often in the first or second named subsection. May recur when secondary concepts are introduced.

**How it works in practice:**

Patashnik (2008, Ch. 2) defines "general-interest reform" with a crisp, portable formulation:

> "By general-interest reform, I mean a non-incremental change of an existing line of policymaking intended to rationalize governmental undertakings or to distribute benefits to some broad constituency."

The definition is one sentence. It does three things at once: specifies the genus ("non-incremental change"), the differentia ("rationalize governmental undertakings or distribute benefits"), and the scope ("some broad constituency"). The reader can now apply the label independently.

Lichterman (2021, Ch. 1) defines "civic action" by specifying what it includes and what it excludes:

> "When people work together, voluntarily, to address problems they think should matter to others, they are engaging in civic action. There are different ways to do civic action. Civic action may or may not be contentious; that is part of what actors decide as they figure out how to address problems."

The definition is deliberately open-ended -- "may or may not be contentious" -- because the book's argument depends on civic action being a bigger conceptual box than "social movement."

Bolton & Thrower (2022, Ch. 2) define "legislative capacity" by decomposing it into two domains:

> "Legislative capacity is separable into two distinct but interrelated domains: policymaking capacity and resource capacity. The former refers to the institutional rules that bind legislatures' power to influence policy and the political assets that facilitate it."

The two-domain structure is itself the theoretical contribution. By defining the concept as a compound, they set up the interactive hypotheses that follow.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Label** -- the term being defined, often italicized or placed in a grammatically prominent position.
2. **Genus** -- what category the concept belongs to.
3. **Differentia** -- what distinguishes it from adjacent concepts.
4. **Boundary clarification** -- what the concept is *not*, or what it does not require.

**Critical rule:** The definition must be quotable. If a reviewer cannot extract the definition in one or two sentences, it is buried and needs to be surfaced.

**Diagnostic:** Can a reader state the definition without looking back at the page?

### 2. LIT_POSITION

**Purpose:** Review an existing body of scholarship and state where the book's argument stands relative to it. The paragraph concedes contributions, then identifies the specific limitation that motivates the book's departure.

**Typical position:** After the opening framing section, typically grouped in a literature engagement section. In Deductive-Formal and Behavioral-Spillover chapters, these paragraphs are relatively compressed. In Interpretive-Conceptual chapters, they can be extended and occupy a large share of the chapter.

**How it works in practice:**

Patashnik (2008, Ch. 2) positions against multiple prior studies in a single paragraph by naming each and identifying the specific point of divergence:

> "While the political sustainability of general-interest reforms has been little-studied, this analysis joins a broader dialogue about the politics of policy stability and change, one to which scholars as diverse as Forrest Maltzman and Charles Shipan, Andrea Campbell, Suzanne Mettler, Jacob Hacker, Terry Moe, Karen Orren and Steven Skowronek, Christopher Berry, Barry Burden and William Howell, and Frank Baumgartner and Bryan Jones have made contributions. My analysis differs in subtle but important ways from these prior studies."

He then proceeds to differentiate from each: "In providing a detailed examination of how the rules, incentives, and norms embedded in reforms channel and constrain political dynamics over extended periods of time, my analysis departs from Maltzman and Shipan's important quantitative study of legal durability."

Lichterman (2021, Ch. 1) positions against the "entrepreneurial actor" model that dominates social movement scholarship:

> "Researchers often assume that social advocates are goal-oriented operatives. Yet the scenarios in the introduction could suggest that housing advocates in Los Angeles were sometimes confused, petty, even incompetent."

The concession ("goal-oriented operatives") is immediately complicated by the empirical anomaly ("confused, petty, even incompetent"), which the entrepreneurial model cannot accommodate.

Tesler (2016, Ch. 2) positions within the racial priming literature to show that Obama's presidency extends existing mechanisms:

> "Prior research suggests that this racialization of issues with no manifest racial content results from mass communications that heighten the association -- either consciously or unconsciously -- between African Americans and political evaluations."

The prior work is credited, then extended: Obama is the new, more powerful form of the racial cue that existing experiments have already documented.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Concession** -- acknowledge what existing work gets right.
2. **Specification** -- name the specific claim or finding being engaged.
3. **Pivot** -- identify the limitation, gap, or assumption that creates room for the book's contribution.

**Critical rule:** Never review literature for its own sake in a theory chapter. Every cited work must earn its place by doing one of three things: providing a building block the theory incorporates, representing a position the theory departs from, or exemplifying a gap the theory fills.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader know why this literature matters *for this book's argument*?

### 3. LIT_GAP

**Purpose:** Identify what prior work misses, overlooks, or cannot explain. This is the pivot paragraph that justifies the theoretical innovation. It converts the literature review from a survey into an argument.

**Typical position:** Immediately after a LIT_POSITION paragraph or sequence. Often the final paragraph of the literature engagement section, serving as a bridge to the framework development section.

**How it works in practice:**

Patashnik (2008, Ch. 2) identifies the gap as a missing temporal dimension:

> "In contrast, almost no scholarship has explored the possible feedback from general-interest reform laws. Yet the sustainability of these reforms hinges on the reactions, expectations, and behavioral change they generate over time."

The gap is named ("almost no scholarship") and the stakes of filling it are made immediate ("the sustainability of these reforms hinges on" this very thing).

Bolton & Thrower (2022, Ch. 2) identify a gap in how capacity is theorized during unified government:

> "Previous studies mainly contend legislatures only engage in constraining activities when they do not share the policy goals of the executive. Consequently, we have little indication as to whether or why legislatures curb executive power when they ostensibly share these goals, notably during unified government."

The gap is that existing theory predicts a null relationship where Bolton & Thrower's framework predicts an active one -- capacity matters even under unified government.

Lichterman (2021, Ch. 1) names the gap as a missing "how" question:

> "The problem is that we need room to imagine different ways of relating to intentions and goals, deciding what counts as a goal, and what counts as winning. Rather than look for universal principles and dilemmas of strategic action, in other words, we can learn what being goal oriented means in different settings."

The gap is not a factual omission but a conceptual limitation: the entrepreneurial actor model forecloses questions about different modes of strategic action.

**Internal structure:**

1. **State the consensus** -- what most scholars assume or conclude.
2. **Name the blind spot** -- what this consensus fails to address.
3. **Assert the consequence** -- why the blind spot matters for understanding the phenomenon.

**Critical rule:** The gap must connect directly to the framework the next section will build. If the gap paragraph identifies a problem that the chapter's theory does not solve, it belongs elsewhere.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader feel the gap as a genuine intellectual problem, not merely a literature lacuna?

### 4. MECHANISM_SPECIFY

**Purpose:** Explain how variables relate causally -- the process or pathway through which an independent variable produces an outcome. This is the core explanatory work of the theory chapter.

**Typical position:** Central to the framework development section. Often presented in a sequence of MECHANISM_SPECIFY paragraphs, each tracing one causal pathway.

**How it works in practice:**

Patashnik (2008, Ch. 2) specifies the mechanism of "creative destruction" by which reforms disrupt existing political orders:

> "Before a reform can create a new order, it must break up the existing one. Joseph A. Schumpeter famously described a process of 'creative destruction,' which occurs when entrepreneurs introduce new products, create new markets, or invent new methods of production."

He then applies the mechanism to policy reform: "Political reformers attack power monopolies and invest in the development of new policy ideas."

Bolton & Thrower (2022, Ch. 2) specify the mechanism by which legislative capacity deters executive unilateralism:

> "Constraint arises from legislatures' powers and resources to restrict discretion for opposed executive actors and to inflict costly retaliatory measures against unilateral actions they find distasteful. The former limits the policy benefits from an order, while the latter deters it in the first place."

The mechanism has two prongs (discretion restriction + retaliation threat) that work together. The paragraph makes both visible.

Lowande (2024, Ch. 2) specifies how symbolic policymaking serves presidential interests even when it fails substantively:

> "Often, no change happens because the action itself had symbolic, political value. It was packaged and enacted to be symbolically effective, not substantive. The president did not order anyone to do anything. He asked for advice, set out broad goals, created unfunded suggestions, or implied that he would like something done, without the real authority to do it."

The mechanism is the decoupling of symbolic and substantive dimensions of executive action.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Name the cause** -- the independent variable or triggering condition.
2. **Trace the pathway** -- the process connecting cause to outcome, step by step.
3. **Identify the outcome** -- the dependent variable or observable consequence.
4. **State the logic** -- why this pathway operates (incentives, constraints, information costs, etc.).

**Critical rule:** A mechanism paragraph must be distinguishable from a mere correlation claim. The reader should be able to reconstruct *why* X produces Y, not merely *that* it does.

**Diagnostic:** Could a skeptical reader draw a causal diagram from this paragraph?

### 5. SCOPE_CONDITION

**Purpose:** State when, where, or under what circumstances the framework applies. Scope conditions bound the theory's claims and preempt the objection that the framework overgeneralizes.

**Typical position:** After the mechanism has been specified. In Deductive-Formal chapters, scope conditions often appear alongside or immediately before hypotheses. In Interpretive-Conceptual chapters, they may be woven throughout the framework development.

**How it works in practice:**

Tesler (2016, Ch. 2) states four scope conditions for the spillover of racialization:

> "All told, then, we should not expect the spillover of racialization to extend into every Obama-era political opinion or every American's political evaluations. Nor would we expect all racial spillover effects to be as strong and as long lasting as others. Rather, the ripest candidates for strong and lasting racial spillover effects were as follows: (1) issues on which Barack Obama takes a visible position that is reinforced over time, (2) issues on which Barack Obama's position is perceived as benefiting blacks, (3) political evaluations in which the pre-Obama effects of racial attitudes were low relative to the impact of party and ideology, and (4) political evaluations that were not already crystallized prior to Obama's presidency."

The numbered list is the most portable device in the chapter. It tells the reader exactly when to expect the effect and when not to.

Bolton & Thrower (2022, Ch. 2) bound their theory by specifying when evasion versus constraint should occur:

> "When legislative capacity is high, we thus expect patterns consistent with constraint -- that is, greater unilateralism during unified government relative to divided. When legislative capacity is lower, however, executives have less fear of reprisal for unilaterally circumventing the statutory process."

The scope condition is the variable (legislative capacity) that flips the prediction from constraint to evasion.

Lowande (2024, Ch. 2) specifies when symbolic action becomes more attractive:

> "As the president's discretion decreases, their actions should become less substantive and more symbolic."

The scope condition is discretion: the theory's predictions reverse depending on how much authority the president actually has.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Negative boundary** -- what the theory does *not* predict.
2. **Conditioning variable** -- the factor that determines whether the mechanism operates.
3. **Positive boundary** -- what the theory *does* predict, given the conditioning variable.

**Critical rule:** Scope conditions must be falsifiable. If a condition is so broad that it can never be violated ("when politics is at work"), it adds no information.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader know when the theory should *not* work?

### 6. HYPOTHESIS_STATE

**Purpose:** Present a formal prediction derived from the theoretical framework. The hypothesis is the portable, testable claim that the empirical chapters will evaluate.

**Typical position:** Late in the framework development section, after mechanisms and scope conditions have been specified. In Deductive-Formal chapters, hypotheses are numbered and set apart visually. In other clusters, predictions may be stated in running prose.

**How it works in practice:**

Bolton & Thrower (2022, Ch. 2) present numbered hypotheses with precise conditional logic:

> "Hypothesis 1: When legislatures have high levels of both policymaking and resource capacities, they place more overall constraints on the executive branch relative to when either is lacking."

> "Hypothesis 2: When legislatures have high levels of both policymaking and resource capacities, they place more constraints on the executive branch as interbranch preference divergence increases. Interbranch disagreement does not impact the level of constraints legislatures place on the executive when they lack either policymaking or resource capacity."

Each hypothesis specifies both what happens when the condition is met and what happens when it is not, making the null prediction explicit.

Lowande (2024, Ch. 2) states predictions in italicized running prose rather than numbered format:

> "*As the president's opposition increases, their actions should become less substantive, and more symbolic.*"

> "*As election day approaches, executive action should become less substantive and more symbolic.*"

The format is less formal but the content is equally precise: variable, direction, and observable implication.

Tesler (2016, Ch. 2) names his core prediction with a label:

> "This hypothesis, which I call the spillover of racialization, therefore suggests that Barack Obama's strong connection to a number of political evaluations should have led to a growing influence of both racial attitudes and race on mass politics during his presidency."

The label ("spillover of racialization") makes the hypothesis citable and portable. It becomes a term of art.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Independent variable** -- named clearly.
2. **Direction** -- whether the effect increases or decreases.
3. **Dependent variable** -- the outcome being predicted.
4. **Conditioning factor** (optional) -- "when" or "given that" clauses.

**Critical rule:** Hypotheses in book-length theory chapters are almost never specified with mathematical precision. They use directional language ("more," "less," "greater") rather than point predictions. The precision comes from the scope conditions.

**Diagnostic:** Could the reader tell you what evidence would falsify this prediction?

### 7. TYPOLOGY_PRESENT

**Purpose:** Introduce a classification, matrix, or taxonomy that organizes the theoretical space. Typologies give the reader a map of possibilities, making the framework portable across cases.

**Typical position:** Mid-chapter, after the mechanisms have been specified and before the hypotheses. The typology often serves as the visual centerpiece of the chapter.

**How it works in practice:**

Patashnik (2008, Ch. 2) presents a 2x2 matrix of post-reform dynamics:

> "This implies a simple two-by-two matrix in which both the stability and cohesion of group identities and the level of investments vary (Figure 2-1). Four post-reform paths can be specified."

The four cells are labeled with vivid shorthand: "Reversal of Reform ('The Empire Strikes Back')," "Erosion of Reform ('Death by a thousand cuts')," "Entrenchment of Reform ('Dug in')," and "Reconfiguration ('Whole new ball game')."

The nicknames make the typology memorable. A reader can carry "Empire Strikes Back" versus "Death by a Thousand Cuts" through the case chapters without returning to the matrix.

Lowande (2024, Ch. 2) presents a typology of symbolic executive actions:

> "Think pieces, non sequiturs, and basket bets -- these are shorthand for the kinds of actions that tend to be more symbolic than they are substantive."

Each type is defined through a paragraph-length vignette: a think piece "orders the development of policies and guidelines, usually through the creation of some new and special commission"; a non sequitur "does not actually address the problem or achieve the stated goal"; a basket bet means to "do something you think will fail, something with long odds, and go down for the sake of appearances."

Smith & King (2024, Ch. 2) present a network-based typology distinguishing "policy alliances" from "institutional orders":

> "We have from the outset of our work defined 'institutional orders' as 'coalitions of state institutions and other political actors and organizations that seek to secure and exercise governing power in demographically, economically, and ideologically structured contexts.'"

The distinction between an alliance that does not control governing institutions and an order that does is the typological spine of the chapter.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Organizing dimensions** -- the axes or criteria that generate the cells.
2. **Cell labels** -- names for each type, ideally vivid and memorable.
3. **Cell descriptions** -- brief characterization of each type's logic.
4. **Mapping to cases** -- which empirical cases fall into which cells (sometimes deferred to later chapters).

**Critical rule:** A typology must have at least one empty or undertheorized cell that the book's cases will populate. If every cell is already well-documented, the typology is a literature review, not a theoretical contribution.

**Diagnostic:** Can the reader place the book's cases into the typology without further instruction?

### 8. EMPIRICAL_PREVIEW

**Purpose:** Signal what the cases, data, or evidence in the empirical chapters will show. The preview paragraph creates anticipation and connects the abstract framework to concrete findings without delivering the full analysis.

**Typical position:** Late in the chapter, often in a penultimate section. In Deductive-Formal and Behavioral-Spillover chapters, this may be a distinct section. In other clusters, it may be woven into the chapter's closing paragraphs.

**How it works in practice:**

Patashnik (2008, Ch. 2) previews the case chapters through his typology:

> "The next five chapters provide a detailed examination of reform experiences in key arenas of American national government."

Bolton & Thrower (2022, Ch. 2) preview the empirical strategy:

> "In the chapters to follow, we empirically evaluate the theory presented here. We test the hypotheses related to legislative constraints by examining statutory discretion and oversight. We then consider executive power by reviewing both presidential and gubernatorial executive orders in separate chapters."

The preview names the dependent variables (discretion, oversight, executive orders) and the levels of analysis (federal and state), giving the reader a map of what is coming.

Tesler (2016, Ch. 2) previews specific chapters by naming the dependent variables they will test:

> "The spillover of racialization hypothesis, as formalized in that chapter, argues that a wide array of Americans' opinions -- including their evaluations of Barack Obama's political rivals (e.g., Mitt Romney) and allies (e.g., Joe Biden), their public policy preferences (e.g., health care), their subjective evaluations of objective economic conditions (e.g., the unemployment rate), their vote choices for Congress, their partisan attachments, and even their impressions of Portuguese water dogs (the breed of the Obama family's dogs, Bo and Sunny) -- should have all become more polarized by racial attitudes and race during the Obama presidency."

The breadth of the list (from health care to Portuguese water dogs) is itself the claim -- the spillover is pervasive.

**Internal structure:**

1. **What is coming** -- which chapters, which cases, which data.
2. **What they will test** -- which hypotheses or framework elements.
3. **Anticipatory signal** -- a hint of the findings without spoiling the analysis.

**Critical rule:** The empirical preview must not pre-empt the empirical chapters. It should create appetite, not satiation. A preview that says "Chapter 4 will show that X causes Y, and Chapter 5 confirms this" eliminates the reader's reason to continue.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader want to turn to the next chapter after reading this paragraph?

### 9. EXAMPLE_ILLUSTRATE

**Purpose:** Use a brief case, vignette, or concrete instance to make an abstract theoretical point tangible. The example is subordinate to the concept -- it illustrates, it does not carry the argument.

**Typical position:** Immediately after a CONCEPT_DEFINE, MECHANISM_SPECIFY, or TYPOLOGY_PRESENT paragraph. The example paragraph gives the abstraction a body.

**How it works in practice:**

Lowande (2024, Ch. 2) illustrates "think pieces" with a concrete presidential action:

> "Every year around the last Thursday in November, the president gathers supporters, staff, and press to pardon a Thanksgiving turkey. This use of presidential power for holiday cheer became a tradition after the Reagan administration and has created good press ever since. Most people do not know these turkeys are bred to be slaughtered. They have terminal health conditions because of their enormous size. All die within months of being pardoned."

The turkey pardon illustrates the decoupling of symbolic and substantive action with dark humor. The reader will not forget the concept.

Patashnik (2008, Ch. 2) illustrates transaction-cost manipulation with a brief case:

> "Consider the case of ERISA. As we shall see, this reform was designed to universalize and raise the funding standards for defined-pension benefits, yet the Pension Benefit Guaranty Corporation was crafted without the authority to impose market-based insurance premiums."

The case is two sentences long. It serves the mechanism, not the other way around.

Bolton & Thrower (2022, Ch. 2) open the chapter with the Zuckerberg hearing to illustrate what low legislative capacity looks like in practice:

> "Senator Orrin Hatch (R-UT) asked, 'How do you sustain a business model in which users do not pay for your service?' -- a strange question given that advertising, Facebook's key source of revenue, was at the center of the hearing."

The absurdity of the question makes the concept of resource capacity vivid before it is formally defined.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Brief setup** -- the case or instance, rendered quickly.
2. **Concrete detail** -- one or two specifics that make the example memorable.
3. **Conceptual link** -- an explicit or implicit connection back to the concept being illustrated.

**Critical rule:** An example paragraph must be short. If the case extends beyond a paragraph, it has become an empirical discussion and belongs in a later chapter.

**Diagnostic:** Does the example help the reader *see* the concept, or does it merely add words?

### 10. FRAMEWORK_CRYSTALLIZE

**Purpose:** Summarize the chapter's theoretical contribution in a single, portable statement. This is the takeaway the reader carries into the empirical chapters. It distills the framework into its most compressed form.

**Typical position:** The final substantive paragraph of the chapter, or the opening paragraph of a concluding subsection. In Deductive-Formal chapters, it often follows the list of hypotheses. In Interpretive-Conceptual chapters, it may restate the new vocabulary as a coherent whole.

**How it works in practice:**

Patashnik (2008, Ch. 2) crystallizes the entire framework in a single sentence:

> "The main lesson is that reform outcomes are never completely settled. Policy reform must be seen as a dynamic process in which forces seeking to protect a reform may be opposed by forces seeking to undo it."

Bolton & Thrower (2022, Ch. 2) crystallize the interactive theory:

> "Overall, when legislatures lack sufficient capacity, executive actors are empowered to act in ways that may make legislative majorities worse off. Once their capacity increases, legislators can constrain executive activities through ex ante and ex post means, both of which are necessary for inducing executive restraint in unilateralism."

The crystallization includes the conditional logic: "when X is lacking ... once X increases." The reader can carry this if-then structure through every empirical chapter.

Lowande (2024, Ch. 2) crystallizes the false-front argument:

> "Some executive actions will fail to move policy, while creating the impression that it does."

One sentence. The book's title, argument, and theoretical contribution are all compressed into fourteen words.

Smith & King (2024, Ch. 2) crystallize their institutional-orders framework:

> "Our modified institutional orders framework, then, embraces more fully than ever the conception of human societies as interinstitutional systems with subsystems of institutional orders, while insisting that all institutional orders contain at least some state institutions; that those state institutions most often should not be conceived as unitary actors comprising a single, distinct institutional order; and that those policy alliances that do not control any state institutions are nonetheless often important subjects of institutionalist analyses."

This is denser than other crystallizations -- appropriate for an Interpretive-Conceptual chapter where the framework's value lies in its conceptual precision.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Restate the core claim** -- the framework in its most compressed form.
2. **Conditional logic** -- the key if-then structure, if applicable.
3. **Forward orientation** -- an implicit or explicit signal that the empirical chapters will now test or apply the framework.

**Critical rule:** The crystallization must be *quotable*. If a reviewer or reader wanted to capture the book's theory in one paragraph, this is the paragraph they should be able to cite. Write it with that standard in mind.

**Diagnostic:** If you copied only this paragraph into a review of the book, would the theory be intelligible?

## Paragraph Sequence Diagrams by Cluster

The following diagrams show the typical order in which paragraph functions appear within each cluster. Not every function appears in every chapter, and the sequences below represent the modal pattern, not a rigid template.

### Deductive-Formal (Bolton & Thrower)

```
EXAMPLE_ILLUSTRATE (opening vignette)
    |
CONCEPT_DEFINE --> CONCEPT_DEFINE (two domains of capacity)
    |
LIT_POSITION --> LIT_GAP
    |
MECHANISM_SPECIFY --> MECHANISM_SPECIFY (ex ante + ex post)
    |
SCOPE_CONDITION
    |
HYPOTHESIS_STATE --> HYPOTHESIS_STATE --> HYPOTHESIS_STATE --> HYPOTHESIS_STATE
    |
EMPIRICAL_PREVIEW
    |
FRAMEWORK_CRYSTALLIZE
```

**Notes:** This cluster is the most linear. Concepts are defined early and precisely. The hypothesis sequence is the chapter's climax, and the typology function is often absent (replaced by the conditional logic of the hypotheses themselves).

### Interpretive-Conceptual (Lichterman; Smith & King)

```
LIT_POSITION --> LIT_POSITION --> LIT_POSITION (extended engagement)
    |
LIT_GAP ("the missing 'how'")
    |
CONCEPT_DEFINE --> CONCEPT_DEFINE (new vocabulary)
    |
EXAMPLE_ILLUSTRATE --> EXAMPLE_ILLUSTRATE (ethnographic vignettes)
    |
MECHANISM_SPECIFY (cultural rather than causal)
    |
CONCEPT_DEFINE (further refinement)
    |
TYPOLOGY_PRESENT (optional; Smith & King use network mapping)
    |
FRAMEWORK_CRYSTALLIZE
```

**Notes:** This cluster spends the most time on literature engagement -- often 40-50% of the chapter. The concept definitions emerge *from* the critique of prior work, not before it. Hypotheses are rare; the portable takeaway is the new vocabulary itself.

### Historical-Institutionalist (Patashnik)

```
CONCEPT_DEFINE ("general-interest reform")
    |
LIT_POSITION (brief)
    |
MECHANISM_SPECIFY --> MECHANISM_SPECIFY --> MECHANISM_SPECIFY
    (information strategies, procedural strategies, compensation tactics)
    |
SCOPE_CONDITION (limitations of each strategy)
    |
MECHANISM_SPECIFY (political structures, creative destruction, policy feedback)
    |
TYPOLOGY_PRESENT (2x2 matrix of post-reform dynamics)
    |
EMPIRICAL_PREVIEW
    |
FRAMEWORK_CRYSTALLIZE
```

**Notes:** The Historical-Institutionalist cluster is distinguished by the sheer number of mechanisms specified. The chapter reads as a systematic inventory of causal pathways. The typology paragraph serves as the chapter's visual centerpiece.

### Behavioral-Spillover (Tesler)

```
EXAMPLE_ILLUSTRATE (opening anecdote: the chimpanzee cartoon)
    |
MECHANISM_SPECIFY (racial priming literature)
    |
LIT_POSITION --> LIT_POSITION (racial cue research)
    |
HYPOTHESIS_STATE ("spillover of racialization")
    |
SCOPE_CONDITION --> SCOPE_CONDITION --> SCOPE_CONDITION --> SCOPE_CONDITION
    (four numbered conditions)
    |
LIT_GAP (anger and polarization -- extending the mechanism)
    |
EMPIRICAL_PREVIEW (measurement and data strategy)
    |
FRAMEWORK_CRYSTALLIZE
```

**Notes:** The Behavioral-Spillover cluster is organized around a single labeled hypothesis that is then bounded by multiple scope conditions. The scope conditions do much of the theoretical work -- they are where the chapter makes its most original contributions.

### Realist-Strategic (Lowande)

```
EXAMPLE_ILLUSTRATE (the Thanksgiving turkey)
    |
LIT_POSITION ("policymaker assumption")
    |
LIT_GAP (symbolic dimension is neglected)
    |
CONCEPT_DEFINE --> CONCEPT_DEFINE ("substantive" vs. "symbolic" dimensions)
    |
TYPOLOGY_PRESENT (think pieces, non sequiturs, basket bets)
    |
EXAMPLE_ILLUSTRATE --> EXAMPLE_ILLUSTRATE --> EXAMPLE_ILLUSTRATE
    (one per type)
    |
MECHANISM_SPECIFY (costs and benefits of each strategy)
    |
SCOPE_CONDITION --> HYPOTHESIS_STATE
SCOPE_CONDITION --> HYPOTHESIS_STATE
SCOPE_CONDITION --> HYPOTHESIS_STATE
    |
EMPIRICAL_PREVIEW
    |
FRAMEWORK_CRYSTALLIZE
```

**Notes:** The Realist-Strategic cluster pairs scope conditions with hypotheses in an alternating pattern. Each new conditioning variable (discretion, opposition strength, election timing) generates its own prediction. The typology appears early and does heavy lifting -- it gives names to phenomena that previously lacked labels.
