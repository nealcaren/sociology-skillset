# Paragraph Functions in Parallel Case Study Chapters

This guide identifies ten paragraph-level functions that recur across parallel case study chapters in political sociology and American politics monographs. Each function has a defined purpose, a characteristic internal structure, and real examples drawn from four books: Brown-Saracino (2018), Mucciaroni (2008), Patashnik (2008), and Kadivar (2022).

Use this guide in two ways: (1) when drafting, to decide what each paragraph needs to accomplish; (2) when revising, to diagnose paragraphs that feel flat, redundant, or out of place.

---

## The Ten Paragraph Functions

### 1. CASE_OPEN

**Purpose:** Establish the case and its stakes in the chapter's first paragraph. The reader must know which case they are entering and why it matters before any analysis begins.

**Typical position:** Paragraph 1. Always.

**How it works across clusters:**

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino, Ithaca):** The author arrives at the field site. The opening is first-person, sensory, and accumulative -- details pile up to convey the city's character before any analytical claim:

> In 2006 I moved to Ithaca, New York, to teach at Cornell University. Before moving, I knew what many northeasterners might know of Ithaca: that it was famous for its steep ravines and gorges; that it was home to Cayuga Lake, Cornell University, and Ithaca College; that visitors flocked to the Moosewood, a natural foods restaurant and cooperative; and that it was remote -- tucked into the Finger Lakes region of upstate New York, more than three and a half hours from New York City.

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino, San Luis Obispo):** The second city chapter opens with place description, then immediately declares the theoretical surprise -- the author expected one pattern and found another:

> After Ithaca, I began research in California, staying in a variety of small homes near downtown across several extended field visits. ... I assumed that my time in San Luis Obispo would affirm the lessons I had learned in Ithaca. ... But I was very wrong.

**Analytical Case (Patashnik, Tax Reform):** The case and its outcome are stated in a single declarative opening:

> Major policy reforms can be unraveled without ever being repealed. A leading example has been the gradual erosion of the Tax Reform Act of 1986 (TRA), which has been justly described as 'one of the most extraordinary economic policy initiatives of the 20th century.'

**Analytical Case (Patashnik, Airline Deregulation):** The opening names the case, describes the pre-reform regime, and arrives at the puzzle:

> The Airline Deregulation Act of 1978 is a leading case of general-interest reform. Between 1938 and 1978, the Civil Aeronautics Board (CAB) regulated the airlines as a quasi-public utility. The agency's mission statement was self-contradictory.

**Biographical-Vignette Case (Mucciaroni, Sodomy Laws):** The opening is a story of a real person harmed by the law:

> On May 18, 1970, James McConnell and Jack Baker applied for a marriage license in Minneapolis. After the two men announced publicly that they were gay, the board of regents of the University of Minnesota voted unanimously to withdraw its offer of a librarian's job to McConnell.

**Mechanism-Tracing Case (Kadivar, South Africa/Poland/Pakistan):** The opening names the three cases and their outcome variation:

> South Africa, Poland, and Pakistan ushered in democratic transitions around the same time. The South African regime started its negotiations with Nelson Mandela and his party in 1990. In Poland, the Communist regime convened the historic roundtables with the opposition in 1989. The Pakistani generals negotiated the terms of transition with Benazir Bhutto, the leader of the opposition, in 1988.

**Mechanism-Tracing Case (Kadivar, Tunisia -- anomalous):** The opening names the anomaly:

> The democratic transition in Tunisia occurred after a short period of protest, but in contrast to Egypt, the transition was completed. ... Exploring the case of Tunisia thus presents an opportunity to investigate alternative pathways for democratic consolidation than those presented in previous chapters.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Case identification** -- name the unit (city, policy, country, issue) immediately.
2. **Stakes or outcome** -- state what happened, or what the reader should care about.
3. **Analytical hook** -- signal why this case is puzzling, interesting, or revelatory.

**Critical rule:** The case must be identified within the first two sentences. A paragraph that opens with three sentences of theoretical framing before naming the case has failed. The reader picks up this chapter to learn about *this case* -- name it immediately.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader know which case they are reading about and why it matters by the end of this paragraph?

### 2. THEORY_CONNECT

**Purpose:** Link this case to the book's comparative framework. Name the theoretical variables and state what the theory predicts (or fails to predict) for this case.

**Typical position:** Paragraph 2--4 in Analytical and Mechanism-Tracing chapters; early subsection in Ethnographic chapters; transition paragraph in Biographical-Vignette chapters.

**How it works across clusters:**

**Analytical Case (Patashnik, Tax Reform):** Theory connection in paragraph 2, stating the verdict through the framework's language:

> The TRA's unraveling was predictable because the reform was built upon weak political foundations. While some hoped the reform's passage would initiate a new era of tax policymaking, the TRA failed to change political incentives. It did not recast institutions to constrain future rent-seekers. ... The TRA also did not generate positive policy feedbacks. ... In sum, the TRA represented a stunning victory of general interests over narrow ones that was driven by elite ideas about good tax policy, but the reform failed to durably reconfigure the political dynamic.

**Analytical Case (Patashnik, Airline Deregulation):** Theory connection in paragraph 3, naming the mechanism:

> The central claim of this chapter is that airline deregulation has endured because policymaking has gradually but unmistakably been reconfigured, making it increasingly costly and unattractive to go back. Because the CAB was phased out of existence through a sunset provision contained in the 1978 Act, the federal government no longer possesses an independent commission with the political autonomy and capacity to supervise routes and prices on a continuous basis.

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino, Ithaca):** Theory connection in the "What They Find in Ithaca" subsection, where the chapter's core concepts are formally introduced:

> This chapter traces how LBQ Ithacans perceive city ecology as producing 'costs' and 'benefits' for social relations. Informants tell me, again and again, that their ability, in Ithaca, to forge ties with heterosexual neighbors and to eschew bonds predicated on shared sexuality threaten a sense of true 'community.' ... Yet, they also propose that Ithaca's large population of LBQ residents, feelings of safety and acceptance, and place narratives about Ithaca as a lefty-bubble promote a strong sense of what one woman refers to as *ambient community*.

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino, San Luis Obispo):** Theory connection through explicit cross-case comparison:

> How can two college towns offer such vast differences in identity and ties? If feelings of acceptance, an abundance of LBQ residents, and talk of the city as a beacon of acceptance produce Ithaca's post-identity politics and ambient community, subtle differences in city ecology propel identity politics and lesbian community in San Luis Obispo.

**Biographical-Vignette Case (Mucciaroni, Military Service):** Theory connection at the transition from background to analysis, using explicit self-referential language:

> What conditions and forces have blocked progress in this area despite these positive developments? Following the kind of analysis presented in the last two chapters, we focus our attention on the preferences of key policymakers and the institutional venue in which they addressed the military ban issue.

**Mechanism-Tracing Case (Kadivar, Ch. 3):** Theory connection as methodological explanation:

> I organize the narratives here according to five major components drawn from the theoretical framework: prodemocracy mobilization and its organizational infrastructure, democratic transition, the effect of the mobilization on leadership change, the effect of mobilization on civil society, and the role of these factors in the survival or failure of the new democracy.

**Mechanism-Tracing Case (Kadivar, Tunisia):** Theory connection through explicit anomaly resolution:

> In previous chapters, I argued that long episodes of prodemocracy mobilization create organizational capacities beneficial for democratic consolidation. The UGTT, however, existed before the advent of the 2011 revolution. ... The fact that the UGTT, a legacy organization from the authoritarian era, played a crucial role in Tunisia's emerging democracy highlights the relationship between the political institutions of the ancien regime and their impact on the postauthoritarian civil society.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Name the framework's variables** -- what the theory says matters.
2. **State the prediction** -- what the theory would expect for this case.
3. **Signal the outcome** -- whether the prediction holds, fails, or is complicated.

**Critical rule:** The theory connection must use the framework's own language. If the book's framework names three variables (institutional reconfiguration, creative destruction, policy feedback), the theory-connection paragraph must name those three variables. Generic phrases like "the theory suggests" without specifying *which* variables are in play fails the function.

**Diagnostic:** Could the reader map this chapter's argument onto the book's framework after reading this paragraph?

### 3. SCENE_SET

**Purpose:** Drop the reader into a specific moment, event, or interaction so they experience the case concretely. Scene-setting paragraphs create the evidentiary texture that distinguishes ethnographic and biographical-vignette chapters from purely analytical ones.

**Typical position:** Opening paragraph in Ethnographic and Biographical-Vignette chapters; subsection openers in Ethnographic chapters; absent in Analytical and Mechanism-Tracing chapters.

**How it works in practice:**

**Brown-Saracino (Ithaca -- Farmers Market discovery):**

> On arrival it seemed nearly every car sported one of two bumper stickers: 'Ithaca is Gorges' or 'Ten Square Miles Surrounded by Reality.' My first night I ate, naturally, at the Moosewood, and found the place dominated by three groups, so distinct that they almost felt like caricatures of themselves: students, academics, and 'hippies,' both aging and youthful alike.

**Brown-Saracino (Ithaca -- LBQ visibility):**

> I watched women holding hands with abandon. Playgrounds often contain two-mom families alongside heterosexual couples. Women with short hair wearing Carhartts and hiking boots work behind co-op and coffee shop counters. These recognizably LBQ individuals appeared nearly everywhere: on the bus to Cornell, on hiking trails, at the co-op, Wegman's, and Staples, behind coffee shop counters, in restaurants, and at the library.

**Brown-Saracino (San Luis Obispo -- community events):**

> Not long after I arrived, lesbian-identified residents of San Luis Obispo and surrounding coastal and ranch towns began to invite me to attend their events, and I found myself attending a monthly lesbian chips 'n' chat event at the Gay and Lesbian Alliance center, a potluck group for 'mature' (read: mostly those over fifty-five years) women, a Sunday night social group that meets at a Mexican restaurant, weekly games at a coastal pool hall, and both formal and impromptu dance parties at downtown bars.

**Mucciaroni (Military -- Michelle Douglas vignette):**

> Michelle Douglas joined the Canadian military in 1986 and graduated at the top of her class in basic training. Douglas became a lieutenant in the Air Force, which assigned her to a 'special investigations' unit whose task, in part, was to root out suspected gays and lesbians. The military investigated Douglas herself in 1988 and found that she had a 'close relationship' with another woman.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Sensory anchor** -- specific physical detail that places the reader (bumper stickers, Carhartts, chips 'n' chat).
2. **Actors in motion** -- people doing specific things in a named place.
3. **Accumulation** -- the scene builds through listed details rather than summary statements.
4. **Unresolved tension** -- the scene raises a question the chapter will answer.

**Critical rule:** The scene must serve the argument. Brown-Saracino's Farmers Market details establish the LBQ population's *ubiquity* -- a theoretical variable. Mucciaroni's Douglas vignette establishes institutional hostility toward gay service members -- the chapter's central puzzle. If you could swap in any other scene without changing the argument, the scene is decorative.

**Diagnostic:** Does this paragraph make the reader *see* a specific moment -- and does that moment connect to the chapter's analytical purpose?

### 4. EVIDENCE_PRESENT

**Purpose:** Present a unit of evidence -- quantitative data, a historical fact, an organizational detail -- and connect it to the chapter's argument.

**Typical position:** Throughout the evidence body (middle 60--70% of the chapter). Every subsection contains multiple evidence-presentation paragraphs.

**How it works across clusters:**

**Analytical Case (Patashnik, Tax Reform -- trend data):**

> According to a careful empirical study by Alison E. Post and Paul Pierson, the tax breaks eliminated in 1986 were broadly similar in terms of their political character to the tax breaks repealed in other years. What made the TRA seem different from prior tax laws was the sheer number of credits and deductions scrapped in 1986. But the TRA did not penetrate into legislative routines any more deeply than other tax laws. After the dust settled, breaks began creeping back into the tax code, shredding the principles of base broadening and horizontal equity almost beyond recognition.

**Analytical Case (Patashnik, Acid Rain -- market data):**

> By any measure, this environmental policy reform has been a stunning success. The sulfur dioxide emissions trading market today is robust, and environmental outcomes have vastly improved. Sulfur dioxide emissions in the United States, which were predicted to climb without the program, have declined by 41 percent since 1980. Signs of recovery are evident in some acid rain sensitive regions.

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino -- census anchoring):**

> Data affirm my armchair observations about the breadth of the LBQ population. By the best measure -- 2000 and 2010 census data on same-sex couples, and US Treasury Department data on married female same-sex couples -- Ithaca has an unusually high and growing proportion of lesbian couples. Indeed, in 2000 and 2010 Tompkins County, of which Ithaca is seat, had the third highest proportion of same-sex female couples of all US counties.

**Mechanism-Tracing Case (Kadivar -- organizational detail):**

> South Africa and Poland are paradigmatic cases: social movements led these countries to a durable democracy, which furnishes an ideal opportunity to observe the mechanisms linking contention and durability. These countries witnessed two of the longest mobilization campaigns for democracy in the twentieth century, and they are useful for precisely this reason.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Evidence statement** -- the data point, fact, or finding, stated with specificity.
2. **Source signal** -- attribution (Post and Pierson; census data; EPA figures).
3. **Analytical interpretation** -- what the evidence means for the chapter's argument.

**Critical rule:** Every evidence paragraph must contain an interpretive sentence. A paragraph that presents data without connecting it to the argument is a data dump. Patashnik's "After the dust settled, breaks began creeping back" turns a statistical finding into an analytical claim about the reform's fragility.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader know both what the evidence shows and what it *means* for the argument?

### 5. QUOTE_INTEGRATE

**Purpose:** Weave interview quotes, primary source quotations, or actor statements into the analytical argument so they serve as evidence rather than decoration.

**Typical position:** Throughout the evidence body. Densest in Ethnographic chapters (30--60 quotes per chapter); present but sparser in Analytical and Biographical-Vignette chapters (15--30); sparsest in Mechanism-Tracing chapters (5--35).

**How it works across clusters:**

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino -- informant voice as collective pattern):**

> Informants tell me, again and again, that their ability, in Ithaca, to forge ties with heterosexual neighbors and to eschew bonds predicated on shared sexuality threaten a sense of true 'community,' by which they mean an inclusive, place-based network of individuals who share a social identity and experience social marginalization.

The phrase "again and again" signals that individual quotes represent a collective pattern; the analytical paraphrase ("by which they mean") translates informant language into scholarly categories.

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino -- individual voice embedded in analytical frame):**

> Yet, they also propose that Ithaca's large population of LBQ residents, feelings of safety and acceptance, and place narratives about Ithaca as a lefty-bubble promote a strong sense of what one woman refers to as *ambient community*: a sense of belonging and connection that arises from informal, voluntary ties with a diverse mix of people with whom one shares place, beliefs, politics, and practices.

The informant's phrase "ambient community" is introduced through attribution ("what one woman refers to as"), then immediately defined in analytical terms.

**Analytical Case (Patashnik -- expert voice as authority):**

> As Yale law professor and former Treasury Department official Michael Graetz writes, 'Tax experts now regard the 1986 act as a promise failed.'

The quotation arrives after several paragraphs documenting erosion; Graetz's authority (Yale, Treasury) reinforces the analytical claim.

**Biographical-Vignette Case (Mucciaroni -- institutional actors' statements as positional evidence):**

> The authorities jailed the men and charged them with violating the Texas Homosexual Conduct law by engaging in 'deviate sexual intercourse, namely anal sex, with a member of the same sex (man)' (quoted in opinion of U.S. Supreme Court Justice Anthony Kennedy for its decision in *Lawrence v. Texas*).

The legal language is quoted verbatim to demonstrate the law's specificity and the institutional apparatus behind it.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Analytical frame** -- establish what the quote will show before introducing it.
2. **Attribution** -- identify the speaker with enough context to signal their authority or representativeness.
3. **The quote** -- as brief as possible while preserving the evidential force.
4. **Interpretive follow-up** -- what the quote demonstrates, in the author's analytical voice.

**Critical rule:** Never let a quote stand alone as a paragraph. Every quote must be framed before and interpreted after. Brown-Saracino's technique of embedding quotes within her own analytical sentences ("what one woman refers to as *ambient community*") is the most efficient integration method.

**Diagnostic:** Is the reader told what to learn from this quote, or left to figure it out?

### 6. SUBSECTION_BRIDGE

**Purpose:** Transition between major subsections of the chapter. Signal what has been established and what comes next.

**Typical position:** Last paragraph of one subsection or first paragraph of the next. Every subsection boundary needs a bridge.

**How it works across clusters:**

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino -- conceptual pivot):**

> The manner of relating with neighbors -- both heterosexual and LBQ -- that these dimensions of city ecology cultivate helps to encourage a second surprise, one that we'll see throughout the book: a transformation of ways of thinking, talking, and feeling about the self and one's membership in a broader social category or group.

This bridge names what has been established ("the manner of relating") and previews what comes next ("a transformation of ways of thinking").

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino -- paradox introduction):**

> The rest of the chapter considers the paradoxical position many in Ithaca find themselves to be in, as they navigate the benefits and burdens of integration and acceptance and, fundamentally, of thinking of themselves as post-identity politics.

**Analytical Case (Patashnik -- from adoption to erosion):**

> Two decades after the 1986 Act, the federal tax code still bears vestiges of reform. For instance, a meaningful zero-bracket amount has been retained for individuals. ... But if the 1986 Act has not been reversed, its accomplishments nonetheless have been eroded.

The "But" pivot is the bridge's hinge -- conceding what survived before documenting what was lost.

**Biographical-Vignette Case (Mucciaroni -- comparative prediction):**

> If our argument about the key role of legislative power and preferences is correct, we should find a very different institutional context in nations that have dropped their bans on gays in their militaries. Comparative analysis may also suggest other institutional reasons for why the ban in the United States persists.

This bridge doubles as a theory connection -- it states a testable prediction that the next subsection will evaluate.

**Mechanism-Tracing Case (Kadivar -- between cases):**

> Poland also presents a case of sustained mobilization and democratic survival, although the effects of its long campaign differ in some ways from South Africa's.

> Pakistan also experienced a democratic transition in the late twentieth century, but in contrast with South Africa or Poland, the transition occurred without a strong prodemocracy mobilization and failed to consolidate.

Both bridges use the same grammatical template: "[Country] also [experienced/presents] ... [but/although] [contrast]." The parallel syntax reinforces the comparative logic.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Summary** -- what the previous subsection established (one sentence).
2. **Pivot** -- a contrastive or consequential connector ("but," "yet," "however," "this raises the question").
3. **Preview** -- what the next subsection will address (one sentence).

**Critical rule:** Subsection bridges must do analytical work, not just announce topics. "The next section discusses the judicial role" is a table-of-contents entry. Mucciaroni's "If our argument ... is correct, we should find" is a bridge that advances the argument.

**Diagnostic:** Does this paragraph move the argument forward, or merely announce a topic change?

### 7. VERDICT_DELIVER

**Purpose:** State the chapter's finding -- what this case demonstrates about the book's theoretical framework. The verdict is the paragraph a reader would quote to summarize the chapter.

**Typical position:** Opening paragraph of a labeled Conclusion section (Analytical and Biographical-Vignette clusters) or final substantive paragraph before the bridge (Ethnographic cluster).

**How it works across clusters:**

**Analytical Case (Patashnik, Tax Reform):**

> While the TRA was a stunning piece of reform legislation, it did not successfully reconfigure the political economy of tax policymaking. Its failures as breakthrough policy are both multiple and mutually reinforcing. The TRA did nothing to disempower tax lobbyists or slow the proliferation of new groups seeking privileged tax treatment. The reform did not raise the political transaction costs to the government of creating or expanding tax loopholes.

**Analytical Case (Patashnik, Airline Deregulation):**

> The airline deregulation case demonstrates that policy reform is a dynamic process in which outcomes are never completely settled. ... Deregulation has stuck in the face of these surprising events not because market competition has delivered only benefits -- although most citizens are better-off -- but because key private actors were forced to accommodate themselves to the new regime.

**Analytical Case (Patashnik, Acid Rain):**

> The reform's sustainability has been as impressive as its original enactment. The emissions trading market has lasted not because it has 'frozen in place' environmental outcomes, or even pollution control technologies, but rather because it has given firms the flexibility to find the cheapest emissions control methods in exchange for the achievement of overall pollution reduction goals.

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino, Ithaca):**

> Finally, the experiences of these individuals in Ithaca helps to confirm what I suspect we already intuitively know: that there is no single, uniform 'lesbian community' to be found in a place like Ithaca. This reminds us that sexual politics and sexual communities are not always mutually constitutive; the ties that LBQ individuals create, and the degree to which they forge ties with other sexual minorities, varies by place.

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino, San Luis Obispo):**

> Being LBQ in San Luis Obispo clearly means something vastly different than it does in Ithaca. The enormous contrast between the residents in these two cities reminds that there is no neat chronological progression out of the closet or away from gay and lesbian identities.

**Biographical-Vignette Case (Mucciaroni, Sodomy Laws):**

> Our examination of the repeal of sodomy laws supports the hypotheses presented in chapter 1 for the most part. Despite the opinion of the public that was, at best, badly split over whether homosexual conduct should be legal, the gay rights movement has achieved total success on the issue of legalizing homosexual conduct. Three reasons account for this outcome: (1) reformers took advantage of variable conditions in the states; (2) the presence of influential stakeholders in the legal profession who favored repeal; and (3) eventual judicial cooperation in the repeal movement.

**Biographical-Vignette Case (Mucciaroni, Military Service):**

> The dispute over gays in the military is our third case of a mismatch between public opinion and public policy. The military case is in crucial respects the reverse image of the sodomy repeal and adoption cases.

**Mechanism-Tracing Case (Kadivar, Ch. 3):**

> The case studies in this chapter reveal the importance of organization building during the years of mobilization and its effect on the transition period and democratic durability. South Africa and Poland emphasized mechanisms of success, and Pakistan illustrated mechanisms of failure.

**Mechanism-Tracing Case (Kadivar, Tunisia):**

> The important role of the UGTT highlights an alternate pathway for democratization that is not marked by a lengthy unarmed mobilization. In Tunisia, a short mobilization liberated a co-opted civil organization from the tight control of the former regime and enabled that organization to play a pivotal role in the transition period.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Signal phrase** -- "This case demonstrates that," "Our examination supports," "The important role of [X] highlights."
2. **The finding** -- stated in the framework's language.
3. **The mechanism or "because" clause** -- what explains the finding.

**Two verdict subtypes:**

| Subtype | Logic | Corpus Example |
|---------|-------|---------------|
| **Enumerated verdict** | Maps findings onto numbered hypotheses or mechanisms | Mucciaroni: "(1)...(2)...(3)..." |
| **Reflective verdict** | States what the case "demonstrates," "reveals," or "reminds" | Brown-Saracino: "The enormous contrast ... reminds that there is no neat chronological progression" |

**Critical rule:** The verdict must be quotable in 1--2 sentences. If the finding is distributed across five paragraphs with no single summary, the reader cannot integrate the case into the comparative argument. Even Brown-Saracino, whose ethnographic chapters are deeply immersive, delivers a quotable verdict sentence.

**Diagnostic:** Could a reader cite this paragraph to summarize the chapter's finding?

### 8. ALTERNATIVE_DISMISS

**Purpose:** Address rival explanations for the case's outcome. Show that the author has considered and rejected alternatives, or identified their limits.

**Typical position:** Within the evidence body (often a dedicated subsection) or in the verdict section as a calibration move.

**How it works across clusters:**

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino, Greenfield -- ruling out chain migration):**

> We learn, for instance, that chain migration -- the movement to a city of one woman, informed by the movement of another before her and so on -- does not neatly account for the emergence of newcomers' post-identity-politics orientation. If it did, we would expect that newcomers or members of the newer migration cohort would be more like Jo and others like her in their approach (i.e., lesbian feminists with strong ties to and identification with Northampton).

**Biographical-Vignette Case (Mucciaroni, Sodomy Laws -- ruling out ideological replacement):**

> Why did the Supreme Court declare state sodomy laws unconstitutional after it concluded just the opposite seventeen years earlier?

This question structures a full subsection ("Explaining the Court's Reversal in Lawrence") that tests three explanations: ideological replacement (rejected by data), generational turnover (supported), and political adjustment (partially supported).

**Mechanism-Tracing Case (Kadivar, Tunisia -- reviewing and critiquing rivals):**

The "Other Explanations" subsection reviews four alternative accounts: (1) apolitical military, (2) different repression histories, (3) stronger civil society (Tocquevillian version), (4) organized working class. Each is summarized and its limits identified before the UGTT explanation is advanced.

**Analytical Case (Patashnik, Acid Rain -- implicit stress test):**

The entire "REREGULATING THE AIRLINE INDUSTRY?" section in the airline chapter functions as an extended alternative-dismiss: each subsection tests whether a specific reversion threat (predatory pricing, passenger service demands, post-9/11 crisis) led to deregulation's undoing. Each is shown to have failed.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Name the alternative** -- identify a plausible rival explanation.
2. **State the prediction** -- what the alternative would expect.
3. **Present the evidence** -- show what actually happened.
4. **Deliver the assessment** -- reject, partially accept, or qualify.

**Critical rule:** Alternative-dismiss paragraphs must be fair. Brown-Saracino gives chain migration a genuine hearing before showing it fails to predict the evidence. Kadivar's "Other Explanations" section summarizes each alternative with enough detail that a proponent would recognize it. Straw-man dismissals undermine credibility.

**Diagnostic:** Would a proponent of the rival explanation feel their view was fairly represented before being assessed?

### 9. CHAPTER_BRIDGE

**Purpose:** Connect to the next chapter or to the book's synthesis. Signal what question remains open or what the next case will show.

**Typical position:** Final paragraph of the chapter. May be absent in some clusters (Patashnik uses implicit bridges).

**How it works across clusters:**

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino, Ithaca -- explicit challenge to universality):**

> The following chapters, though, give pause to any notion of the universality of this shift for LBQ residents and others, as well as about the precise relation between such shifts and orientations to and practices of community. Despite the sense articulated by many Ithacans that they are caught in a moment of broad and universal transformation, Ithaca is one way, but far from the only way.

**Ethnographic Case (Brown-Saracino, Greenfield -- bridge to synthesis):**

> Given what Greenfield reveals about how identity cultures and ties respond to local conditions, we should not anticipate that in twenty years or even two, Portland's LBQ population will rely on hyphenated identities, or that San Luis Obispo will always possess a stalwart lesbian community. When local conditions change, identities and ties change, too. ... It is to the question of precisely how city features shape these interactions, and, ultimately, self-understanding, that the next and final chapter turns.

**Mechanism-Tracing Case (Kadivar, Ch. 3 -- explicit sequential bridge):**

> But how does a democratic transition unfold following short bursts of mass mobilization? In the next two chapters, I examine the failed and successful transitions in Egypt and Tunisia, respectively, to tackle this question.

**Biographical-Vignette Case (Mucciaroni -- bridge carried by the next chapter's opening):**

Chapter 7's opening performs the bridge for Chapter 6:
> The three preceding chapters demonstrated the critical role of institutions in the legalization of homosexual conduct and gay adoption and in the failure to lift the ban on gays in the military. ... What about the policy responses to gay rights advocates' demands for same-sex marriage, civil unions, employment nondiscrimination, and hate crimes legislation?

**Analytical Case (Patashnik -- no explicit bridge):**

Patashnik's case chapters end with their verdict and a closing quotation. There is no transition sentence. The comparative framework, established in the book's introduction, provides the implicit bridge.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Concede what has been shown** -- a compressed summary of this chapter's finding.
2. **Name what remains** -- the question this chapter cannot answer, or the pattern this case cannot generalize.
3. **Point forward** -- either name the next case explicitly or frame the question it will address.

**Critical rule:** Bridges are most important for first and middle cases, where the reader needs guidance about where the argument is heading. Last cases can close on the verdict without a bridge, since the next chapter is typically the book's conclusion.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader know where the argument goes from here?

### 10. POSITION_SIGNAL

**Purpose:** Announce the chapter's position in the comparative architecture: first case, middle case, last case, anomalous case, or negative case. This signal tells the reader how to interpret the chapter's evidence relative to the rest of the book.

**Typical position:** Within the first page, often embedded in the CASE_OPEN or THEORY_CONNECT paragraph.

**How it works across clusters:**

**First case (Brown-Saracino, Ithaca):** Position signaled implicitly through the framing of discovery -- the author is encountering the phenomenon for the first time, and so is the reader:

> However, what neither the data nor feel-good stories like the Advocate's could tell me is why Ithaca possesses a disproportionately high number of lesbian couples. These sources communicate even less about what it feels like to be LBQ in Ithaca.

The question-posing framing signals that this is the foundational case.

**Second case / surprise case (Brown-Saracino, San Luis Obispo):** Position signaled through explicit expectation violation:

> I assumed that my time in San Luis Obispo would affirm the lessons I had learned in Ithaca. ... But I was very wrong. San Luis Obispo's LBQ residents consistently challenged my assumption that LBQ individuals are moving beyond identity politics, that Ithaca's norm was a universal trend.

**Negative case (Mucciaroni, Military):** Position signaled through explicit contrast with prior chapters:

> The dispute over gays in the military is our third case of a mismatch between public opinion and public policy. The military case is in crucial respects the reverse image of the sodomy repeal and adoption cases.

**Anomalous case (Kadivar, Tunisia):** Position signaled with the word "anomalous" itself:

> Exploring the case of Tunisia thus presents an opportunity to investigate alternative pathways for democratic consolidation than those presented in previous chapters.

**Anomalous case (Brown-Saracino, Greenfield):** Position signaled through explicit complication of prior findings:

> This book has already sketched how LBQ residents respond to the ecology of the city in which they live. ... In Greenfield, however, something different is found: there is more than one identity culture.

**Last case / strongest demonstration (Patashnik, Acid Rain):** Position signaled through superlative:

> The emissions trading program constitutes a case of successful policy reconfiguration -- the strongest form of reform sustainability.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Reference to prior cases** -- name or imply what has come before.
2. **This case's distinctive contribution** -- what it adds, contradicts, or complicates.
3. **Interpretive instruction** -- tell the reader whether to expect confirmation, surprise, or extension.

**Critical rule:** Position signals are not optional. Without them, the reader processes each chapter as an isolated essay rather than as a unit in a comparative architecture. The strongest position signals use explicit comparative language: "reverse image" (Mucciaroni), "far from the only way" (Brown-Saracino), "anomalous case" (Kadivar), "strongest form" (Patashnik).

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader know, within the first page, how this case relates to the cases that came before?

## Paragraph Sequences by Cluster

### Analytical Case

> Opens with outcome, immediately connects to theory, then presents evidence domain by domain.

```
CASE_OPEN (outcome stated) --> THEORY_CONNECT --> EVIDENCE_PRESENT (x3-5 per subsection)
--> SUBSECTION_BRIDGE --> EVIDENCE_PRESENT (x3-5) --> ... --> VERDICT_DELIVER
--> ALTERNATIVE_DISMISS (if applicable)
```

### Ethnographic Case

> Opens with arrival, accumulates scenes, introduces theory through a dedicated subsection.

```
CASE_OPEN (first-person arrival) --> SCENE_SET --> SCENE_SET --> EVIDENCE_PRESENT (census/data)
--> THEORY_CONNECT --> SCENE_SET --> QUOTE_INTEGRATE (x3-5) --> SUBSECTION_BRIDGE
--> SCENE_SET --> QUOTE_INTEGRATE (x3-5) --> ... --> VERDICT_DELIVER --> CHAPTER_BRIDGE
```

### Biographical-Vignette Case

> Opens with human stories, pivots to analysis, tests hypotheses in sequence.

```
CASE_OPEN (vignette 1) --> SCENE_SET (vignette 2) --> POSITION_SIGNAL ("As these stories illustrate...")
--> EVIDENCE_PRESENT (historical) --> SUBSECTION_BRIDGE --> THEORY_CONNECT
--> EVIDENCE_PRESENT (x3-5 per subsection) --> ALTERNATIVE_DISMISS --> VERDICT_DELIVER
```

### Mechanism-Tracing Case

> Opens with case-selection rationale, applies template, enumerates mechanisms.

```
CASE_OPEN (case-selection) --> THEORY_CONNECT (methodological) --> POSITION_SIGNAL
--> EVIDENCE_PRESENT (template subsection 1) --> EVIDENCE_PRESENT (template subsection 2)
--> ... --> VERDICT_DELIVER (mechanism enumeration) --> CHAPTER_BRIDGE
```

## Paragraph-Level Diagnostic Checklist

### Opening (Paragraphs 1--3)

| # | Question | If "no" |
|---|----------|---------|
| 1 | Can the reader identify the case by the end of paragraph 1? | Name the case in the first two sentences. |
| 2 | Does the reader know the chapter's claim by the end of paragraph 2--3? | Add a claim-announcement sentence. |
| 3 | Is the case's position in the comparative framework signaled? | Add a POSITION_SIGNAL move. |
| 4 | If using a scene, does it connect to the chapter's argument? | Replace with a scene that serves the analysis. |

### Middle (Evidence Body)

| # | Question | If "no" |
|---|----------|---------|
| 5 | Does every evidence paragraph contain an interpretive sentence? | Add analytical follow-up to each data point. |
| 6 | Is every quote framed before and interpreted after? | Add analytical framing around standalone quotes. |
| 7 | Do subsection bridges advance the argument, not just announce topics? | Rewrite bridges to include analytical content. |
| 8 | Does each subsection have an analytical purpose (not just a topic)? | Rename subsections to signal arguments. |

### Closing (Verdict and Bridge)

| # | Question | If "no" |
|---|----------|---------|
| 9 | Is the verdict quotable in 1--2 sentences? | Rewrite with a signal phrase and explicit claim. |
| 10 | Does the verdict use the framework's own language? | Map findings onto the theory's variables. |
| 11 | Has the chapter acknowledged limits or alternatives? | Add a calibration paragraph. |
| 12 | Does the reader know where the argument goes next? | Add a bridge sentence. |

### Global Checks

| # | Question | If "no" |
|---|----------|---------|
| 13 | Can you label every paragraph with one of the ten functions? | Any paragraph resisting labeling is likely doing two things (split) or nothing (cut). |
| 14 | Are there three or more consecutive paragraphs of the same function without development? | Vary the function sequence or merge paragraphs. |
| 15 | Does the paragraph sequence match a recognizable cluster pattern? | If idiosyncratic, check that each transition is motivated. |
