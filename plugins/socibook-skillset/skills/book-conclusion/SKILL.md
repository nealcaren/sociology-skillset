# Book Conclusion

You help researchers write **conclusion chapters for academic monographs**. Given a book project memo, manuscript draft, or equivalent description of the project and its empirical chapters, you guide users through selecting a conclusion style, structuring the chapter, and drafting publication-ready prose.

## File Management

This skill uses git to track progress. Before modifying any output file at a new phase:
1. Stage and commit current state: `git add [files] && git commit -m "book-conclusion: Phase N complete"`
2. Then proceed with modifications.

Do NOT create version-suffixed copies (e.g., `-v2`, `-final`, `-working`). The git history serves as the version trail.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when the researcher has:
- A completed or near-complete manuscript (empirical chapters are drafted or outlined in detail)
- A written introduction (the conclusion must callback to it)
- A defined book argument and evidentiary base
- Need to write or substantially revise the conclusion chapter

This skill assumes the book's argument has been made in the preceding chapters. The task is crafting the conclusion that synthesizes, broadens, and projects -- not one that introduces the framework for the first time.

## Overview: What a Book Conclusion Does

A book conclusion is not a summary. It performs a distinct set of functions that no empirical chapter can:

- **Restates the thesis** in light of the accumulated evidence, not as repetition but as a verdict earned by the preceding chapters
- **Synthesizes across cases** to produce comparative insights unavailable in any single chapter
- **Introduces new material** -- every conclusion in the corpus does this; the conclusion is not a closed system
- **Shifts scope** beyond the book's empirical boundaries to claim broader significance
- **Shifts register** from analytical to normative, prescriptive, or evaluative
- **Looks forward** -- projecting, prescribing, or posing questions for the future
- **Calls back** to the introduction, creating structural closure

Target length: **5,000--10,000 words** (corpus median: ~7,750). The conclusion is typically 50--70% as long as the introduction (median ratio 1:1.7, conclusion to introduction).

## The Eight Universal Moves

Every book conclusion, regardless of cluster, executes these moves. They appear in more than 80% (18+) of the 22 conclusions. Their order and emphasis vary by cluster, but omitting the first five produces a structurally incomplete conclusion.

| # | Move | Frequency | Function | Diagnostic Question |
|---|------|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Thesis restatement** | 22/22 (100%) | Restate the book's central argument, usually in the first 1--3 paragraphs | Can the reader identify the restated thesis without referring back to the introduction? |
| 2 | **Callback to introduction** | 22/22 (100%) | Explicitly revisit concepts, puzzles, or framing devices from the introduction | Does the conclusion create structural closure by returning to the introduction's central question, analytical framework, or opening anecdote? |
| 3 | **Scope shift beyond cases** | 22/22 (100%) | Broaden the argument beyond the book's empirical scope | Does the conclusion claim significance beyond the specific cases examined? |
| 4 | **New material introduction** | 22/22 (100%) | Introduce material not found in the empirical chapters | Does the conclusion contain at least one element -- a new case, updated data, a new theoretical connection, a prescriptive framework -- that is not available in any prior chapter? |
| 5 | **Normative tone shift** | 22/22 (100%) | Shift from analytical to normative, prescriptive, or evaluative register | Does the conclusion's voice differ from the empirical chapters in a way that signals "what this means" rather than "what this shows"? |
| 6 | **Forward-looking element** | 20/22 (91%) | Include prospective speculation, a research agenda, policy prescriptions, or conditional projections | Does the conclusion look ahead -- to future research, future politics, or future applications of the framework? |
| 7 | **Cross-case synthesis** | 19/22 (86%) | Produce a new comparative synthesis not available in any single chapter | Does the conclusion contain a comparative table, typology, matrix, or set of meta-level propositions that integrates findings across cases? |
| 8 | **Broader literature engagement** | 19/22 (86%) | Engage a scholarly literature beyond the book's primary disciplinary conversation | Does the conclusion connect the book's findings to at least one scholarly conversation not centrally addressed in the empirical chapters? |

**Examples of thesis restatement:**
- Patashnik 2008: "The enactment of a policy reform, then, signals not the end of the political story, only the start of a new chapter."
- Kadivar 2022: "Why do some young democracies fail and others survive? This book proposes that..."
- Brown-Saracino 2018: "we make places, but places also... make us."

**Callback types:** (a) returning to the introduction's central question, (b) revisiting the analytical framework, (c) structural parallels (e.g., opening anecdote mirrored by closing anecdote).

**Scope shift types (from most to least common):**
- From cases to general theory (20/22 -- 91%)
- From analytical to normative/prescriptive (20/22 -- 91%)
- Temporal extension beyond empirical period (15/22 -- 68%)
- From specific policy domain to democratic theory (14/22 -- 64%)
- From national to comparative/international (8/22 -- 36%)
- From one identity/issue to multiple (7/22 -- 32%)
- Methodological/epistemological broadening (4/22 -- 18%)

**New material types (from most to least common):**
- Cross-case synthesis devices (19/22 -- 86%)
- Prescriptive/strategic frameworks (16/22 -- 73%)
- New empirical cases or vignettes (15/22 -- 68%)
- Engagement with new theoretical literatures (14/22 -- 64%)
- Prospective/forward-looking projections (12/22 -- 55%)
- Contemporary event updates (9/22 -- 41%)

## The Five Conclusion Clusters

### Cluster 1: The Policy Architect

**Defining logic:** Synthesize findings and prescribe strategies for policymakers or practitioners. The conclusion's center of gravity is *what should be done*.

**Defining characteristics:** Thesis restatement opening; heavy cross-case synthesis; extended prescriptive sections (strategies, tactics, reform proposals); moderate length; moderate citation density; forward-looking application close.

**Typical structural sequence:**
1. Thesis restatement + case inventory
2. Systematic synthesis (often with new table or matrix)
3. Policy/strategic prescriptions (the conclusion's largest section)
4. Forward-looking application

**Opening move:** Thesis restatement or case inventory (Type A)
**Closing move:** Forward-looking application or contribution statement

| Benchmark | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Word count | 6,000--10,000 |
| Subsections | 4--8 named sections |
| Citation density | Moderate |
| New material | Primarily prescriptive frameworks and cross-case synthesis devices |

**Exemplar books:**
- **Patashnik 2008:** Five reform strategies, Table 9-1, cap-and-trade application. Opens: "The enactment of a policy reform, then, signals not the end of the political story, only the start of a new chapter." Closes by applying the framework to cap-and-trade: "Reform sustainability requires forethought -- but it is not beyond reach."
- **Patashnik 2023:** Four strategic options, five tactical recommendations, "coming backlash?" speculation. Opens with ACA, NCLB, and NAFTA case inventory. Closes with scholarly contribution statement: "The study of backlash politics thus expands and refines our understanding of the multiple ways in which 'new policies create a new politics.'"
- **Bolton & Thrower 2022:** Congressional reform prescriptions, OTA analysis, staffing recommendations. Opens by invoking Madison and the constitutional framers. Closes with title-echo aphorism: "Indeed, checks are in the balance."

**Distinguishing features:** Unlike other clusters, the Policy Architect devotes the conclusion's largest sections to prescriptions. The new material is predominantly prescriptive rather than empirical or theoretical. The tone shift is toward a policy advisory register.

---

### Cluster 2: The Ethnographic Bookend

**Defining logic:** Extend the story rather than abstract from it. Return to the field -- to characters, scenes, and voices from the empirical chapters -- and use narrative continuity to deliver the theoretical payoff.

**Defining characteristics:** Opens with an anecdote (often a callback to an earlier fieldwork scene or a new vignette); shorter length; sparse to moderate citations; humanizing or reflective close; new material includes follow-up interviews or updated fieldwork data.

**Typical structural sequence:**
1. Anecdote or fieldwork scene (new or callback)
2. Theoretical synthesis (compact)
3. Implications for practitioners or the broader public
4. Humanizing or reflective close

**Opening move:** Anecdote/fieldwork scene (Type B)
**Closing move:** Thesis crystallization or conditional optimism

| Benchmark | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Word count | 3,500--6,000 |
| Subsections | 2--4 named sections |
| Citation density | Low to moderate |
| New material | Follow-up conversations, updated fieldwork data, new narrative vignettes |

**Exemplar books:**
- **Ternullo 2024:** Returns to Isaac and Larry; summer 2022 data; humanizing close. Opens: "in august 2021, the 2020 presidential election was in the rearview mirror, Joe Biden was installed in the White House... That month, I spoke to Isaac from Motorville." Closes with thesis crystallization: "the product of an often-invisible web of social organization, social structure, and party politics that weave all Americans into the polity."
- **Fisher 2019:** Returns to Women's March; 2019 survey data; conditional optimism. Opens with meeting Linda Sarsour at the CNN studios. Closes: "If successful, it will leave as its legacy a lasting root structure when the 2020 election is behind us."
- **Abrams 2022:** Movement accomplishments inventory; forward-looking uncertainty. Opens with lessons from undocumented resistance in Arizona. Closes: "the ability to perceive, reflect, and adapt... exemplifies the strength that undocumented activists will carry with them as they move into an uncertain future."

**Distinguishing features:** These are the shortest conclusions. They privilege narrative continuity over theoretical ambition. The new material is experiential rather than analytical. The tone stays closest to the empirical chapters. These conclusions feel like an extension of the book's fieldwork.

---

### Cluster 3: The Theory Builder

**Defining logic:** Deliver the theoretical proposition the empirical chapters have been building toward. The conclusion is where the book's conceptual contribution crystallizes.

**Defining characteristics:** Opens with a broadening philosophical or normative claim; high citation density; engages rival theories explicitly; introduces new theoretical propositions; pronounced tone shift toward abstract/critical register; the conclusion's center of gravity is *theoretical contribution*.

**Typical structural sequence:**
1. Broadening normative or epistemological opening
2. Engagement with rival theories or scholarly literatures
3. New theoretical proposition (the conclusion's main deliverable)
4. Research agenda or field-expanding call

**Opening move:** Broadening normative claim (Type C) or epistemological framing
**Closing move:** Field-expanding call or epistemological reflection

| Benchmark | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Word count | 7,000--13,000 |
| Subsections | 4--5 named sections |
| Citation density | High |
| New material | Primarily theoretical propositions and engagement with new literatures |

**Exemplar books:**
- **Vogler 2021:** Sexuality as technology; racial formation comparison; STS engagement. Opens: "Sorting Sexualities has argued that the techno-legal classification practices that state actors engage in naturalize social distinctions..." Closes: "Sexuality as a technology of social organization, domination, and resistance is everywhere. Our job is to start recognizing it."
- **Lichterman 2021:** Dewey's pragmatism; epistemological defense; researcher-practitioner dialogue. Opens with a broadening question about civic action research worldwide. Closes: "We need people who figure out what civic action can and cannot do to bend history's arc... There is a lot to do."
- **Brown-Saracino 2018:** Differentiated social geography; urban ecology theory; assimilation critique. Opens: "we live in a fascinating and contradictory moment. Much of american culture has taken vast strides in awareness about and acceptance of people who live outside the sexual norm." Closes with thesis crystallization: "we make places, but places also -- and far more than we might like to believe -- make us."

**Distinguishing features:** These conclusions read like the theoretical chapter the book has been building toward. They are the most heavily cited conclusions. The tone is the most intellectually ambitious and the most different from the empirical chapters. They tend to come from qualitative/interpretive books where the theoretical payoff is saved for last.

### Cluster 4: The Democratic Reckoning

**Defining logic:** Reckon with a political crisis or democratic threat. Introduce substantial new empirical material to show how the political landscape has shifted since the fieldwork period, then broaden to claims about democratic health.

**Defining characteristics:** Opens with a scene of political crisis or democratic threat; introduces substantial new empirical material (often current events post-fieldwork); extended scope shift to democratic theory and normative claims; moderate to high citations; pronounced tone shift toward urgency; closes with a call to action or cautious hope.

**Typical structural sequence:**
1. Scene-setting with current crisis or new case
2. Summary of findings
3. Extended new empirical material (elections, crises, policy developments)
4. Broadening to democratic theory / democratic health
5. Call to action or cautiously hopeful close

**Opening move:** Scene-setting with current events (Type B) or broadening democratic claim (Type C)
**Closing move:** Call to action or cautious hope

| Benchmark | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Word count | 5,000--27,000 (high variance) |
| Subsections | 5--14+ named sections |
| Citation density | Moderate to high |
| New material | Current events, election results, hate crime data, international comparisons, future scenarios |

**Exemplar books:**
- **Tesler 2016:** Ferguson opening; "most-racial" verdict; democratic implications. Opens: "For the second consecutive summer of Barack Obama's second term in the White House, the country's precarious racial fault line ruptured over the fatal shooting of an unarmed black teenager." Closes: "Obama's presidency should stand out as yet another profound and sobering reminder of the ongoing importance of race in America."
- **Grumbach 2023:** Florida Amendment 4, COVID/Cuomo; fiscal federalism prescriptions; pro-democracy coalition call. Opens with case inventory of nationalization argument. Closes: "pro-democracy coalitions... should use the power they gain at the national level to shift authority upward."
- **Han et al. 2021:** One PHX ID collapse; democratic fragility; "Our democracy depends on it." Opens: "Democracy, demos kratia, is rooted in the idea that people have some control over the basic conditions and laws that rule our lives." Closes: "Our democracy depends on it."
- **Smith & King 2024:** 2022 elections; global nationalism; six future scenarios; hate crime data. The longest conclusion in the corpus (~27,000 words, a two-chapter conclusion). Closes: "there is no reason to conclude that these efforts must fail. To the contrary, there is every reason to try to help them succeed."

**Distinguishing features:** These conclusions are animated by a sense of political urgency. They introduce the most new empirical material because the political landscape has changed since the fieldwork period. The scope shift is always toward "what this means for democracy." They differ from Policy Architects in that their prescriptions are more about democratic health than specific policy design, and they differ from Theory Builders in that their primary register is political rather than philosophical.

---

### Cluster 5: The Institutional Historian

**Defining logic:** Extend a historical or institutional narrative into the present moment. Trace what happened to the institutions under study after the book's main empirical period, and deliver a diagnostic assessment.

**Defining characteristics:** Opens with a counterintuitive synthesis or intellectual reorientation; introduces new archival or historical material that extends the book's timeline; moderate length; moderate citation density; closes with a diagnostic warning or intellectual challenge rather than optimism or a call to action.

**Typical structural sequence:**
1. Counterintuitive thesis synthesis
2. Extended historical or institutional narrative (often extending forward from the book's endpoint)
3. Assessment of contemporary institutional developments
4. Diagnostic or cautionary close

**Opening move:** Thesis restatement as counterintuitive synthesis (Type A)
**Closing move:** Diagnostic pessimism or warning

| Benchmark | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Word count | 5,000--12,000 |
| Subsections | 4--7 named sections |
| Citation density | Moderate |
| New material | Archival documents, legal decisions, legislative developments, institutional analysis |

**Exemplar books:**
- **Dearborn 2021:** Unitary executive synthesis; Ehrlichman memo; CFPB decision. Opens: "The twentieth-century shift toward presidential government was a remarkable achievement, a veritable reinvention of the American Constitution. The whole story is counterintuitive." Closes: "If we have lost that capacity, we may be in real trouble."
- **SoRelle 2020:** FDR/Obama frame; CFPB survey data; Mulvaney takeover. Opens with the Time magazine cover superimposing Obama onto FDR. Closes: "the outcome of today's battles will make a lasting impression..."
- **Mettler 2011:** Thompson family anecdote; rubric for reform; "reinvigorating democracy." Opens with the Thompson family in Overland Park, Kansas. Closes: "Only by making government visible and ensuring that it promotes social equity can we reinvigorate democracy and revitalize citizenship."
- **Lowande 2024:** McGeachin/Idaho story; rhetorical opening on presidential power. Opens: "What is it, exactly, that makes presidents powerful?" Closes with narrative vignette punchline: "I am one heartbeat away from the governor's chair."

**Distinguishing features:** These conclusions extend a historical or institutional narrative into the present. They are less oriented toward practitioner advice (vs. Policy Architect) and less oriented toward abstract theory (vs. Theory Builder). Their new material tends to be archival or institutional rather than ethnographic or statistical. The emotional register tends toward sobriety or warning rather than hope.

## Cluster Decision Tree

Use this tree to select the right cluster. Work through each question in order.

### Question 1: What is the primary PURPOSE of this conclusion?

- **To synthesize findings and prescribe strategies for policymakers/practitioners** --> go to Question 2A
- **To extend the narrative with new fieldwork or ethnographic data** --> go to Question 2B
- **To build new theory that the empirical chapters have been preparing for** --> go to Question 2C
- **To reckon with a democratic crisis or political urgency** --> go to Question 2D
- **To trace institutional development into the present moment** --> go to Question 2E

### Question 2A: Is the conclusion extending a historical/institutional narrative into the present?

- **YES** --> **Institutional Historian** (with Policy Architect elements). Examples: SoRelle, Bolton & Thrower.
- **NO** --> **Policy Architect**. Examples: Patashnik 2008, Patashnik 2023.

### Question 2B: Is the conclusion shorter than 6,000 words with few new theoretical claims?

- **YES** --> **Ethnographic Bookend**. Examples: Ternullo, Fisher, Abrams.
- **NO** --> Does it build substantial new theory from the ethnographic data?
  - **YES** --> **Theory Builder** (with ethnographic opening). Examples: Lichterman, Brown-Saracino.
  - **NO** --> **Ethnographic Bookend**.

### Question 2C: Is the conclusion heavily cited (20+ external references)?

- **YES** --> **Theory Builder**. Examples: Vogler, Lichterman, Brown-Saracino.
- **NO** --> Is the primary concern democratic health/crisis?
  - **YES** --> **Democratic Reckoning**.
  - **NO** --> **Policy Architect** (with theoretical ambition).

### Question 2D: Does the conclusion introduce substantial new current-events material?

- **YES** --> **Democratic Reckoning**. Examples: Tesler, Grumbach, Han et al., Smith & King.
- **NO** --> Does it end with prescriptions or a research agenda?
  - **YES** --> **Policy Architect** or **Theory Builder**.
  - **NO** --> **Democratic Reckoning** (compact variant).

### Question 2E: Does it feature archival/legal/legislative material extending the timeline?

- **YES** --> **Institutional Historian**. Examples: Dearborn, SoRelle, Mettler, Lowande.
- **NO** --> **Policy Architect**.

### Quick Heuristic

Ask yourself three questions about your conclusion:

1. **What opens it?** Anecdote = Bookend or Reckoning; Thesis restatement = Architect or Historian; Philosophy = Theory Builder.
2. **What is the largest new section about?** Prescriptions = Architect; Theory = Builder; Current events = Reckoning; New fieldwork = Bookend; Institutional narrative = Historian.
3. **What is the emotional register of the close?** Hope/optimism = Bookend or Reckoning; Urgency/call-to-action = Reckoning or Builder; Strategy/advice = Architect; Warning/sobriety = Historian.

## Structural Section Sequences

### The Modal Pattern

The most common structural sequence across the 22 conclusions is:

1. **Opening frame** (thesis restatement OR anecdote/scene)
2. **Synthesis of findings** (cross-case summary, often with new comparative table or matrix)
3. **Qualifications / caveats** (acknowledging limits of the argument)
4. **Theoretical broadening** (connecting to wider scholarly literature)
5. **Policy/practical implications** (prescriptive section)
6. **Forward-looking close** (prospective speculation or call to action)

### Variant A: The Practitioner-Oriented Conclusion

Used by: Patashnik 2008, Patashnik 2023, Bolton & Thrower, Kadivar, Han et al.

1. Thesis restatement + case inventory
2. Systematic synthesis of causal mechanisms
3. Policy prescription / strategic recommendations (substantial section)
4. Forward-looking application or call to action

### Variant B: The Ethnographic Bookend

Used by: Ternullo, Fisher, Mettler, Abrams, Lichterman

1. Anecdote or fieldwork scene (new or callback)
2. Theoretical synthesis
3. Practitioner dialogue or implications
4. Humanizing/normative close

### Variant C: The Theory-Building Conclusion

Used by: Brown-Saracino, Vogler, Rodriguez-Muniz, Lichterman, Han et al.

1. Broadening philosophical/normative claim
2. Systematic engagement with rival theories
3. New theoretical proposition
4. Research agenda or field-expanding call

### Variant D: The Extended Epilogue

Used by: Smith & King, SoRelle, Lowande, Dearborn

1. Theoretical synthesis of prior chapters
2. Extended new empirical material (new cases, updated data, current events)
3. Scenarios or future projections
4. Normative close

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid these failure modes. They are derived from what the 22-book corpus consistently does NOT do.

### 1. The pure summary conclusion

No conclusion in the corpus merely recaps the empirical chapters. Every single one (22/22) introduces new material -- new cases, new prescriptions, new theoretical connections, or updated data. A conclusion that only summarizes what came before has failed its genre function.

### 2. The conclusion that ignores its introduction

Every conclusion (22/22) explicitly callbacks to the introduction. A conclusion that reads as if the introduction does not exist -- with no return to the opening puzzle, framing device, or central question -- breaks the structural closure the genre requires.

### 3. The conclusion that stays in the analytical register

Every conclusion (22/22) shifts tone toward normative, prescriptive, or evaluative language. A conclusion that maintains the same detached analytical voice as the empirical chapters has not done its work. The conclusion is where the author is permitted -- expected -- to say what the findings mean for the world, not just for the theory.

### 4. The conclusion that introduces no new material

All 22 conclusions introduce material not found in the empirical chapters. This is a universal genre feature. A conclusion that draws only from material already presented is too conservative. Typical new material includes: cross-case synthesis devices (86%), prescriptive frameworks (73%), new empirical cases (68%), engagement with new literatures (64%).

### 5. The conclusion that never looks forward

Twenty of 22 conclusions (91%) include a forward-looking component. A conclusion that is entirely retrospective -- that never speculates about the future of the subject, proposes a research agenda, or offers conditional projections -- misses one of the genre's near-universal moves.

### 6. The conclusion that tries to do everything

Smith & King's ~27,000-word two-chapter conclusion is the outlier, not the model. The median is ~7,750 words. A conclusion that attempts comprehensive prescriptions AND deep theory-building AND extended new empirical material AND a research agenda will lose focus. Choose a primary cluster and let it discipline what gets included.

### 7. The conclusion that opens with methodology or literature review

No conclusion in the corpus opens with a literature review or a methods discussion. They open with thesis restatements (32%), anecdotes or scenes (32%), broadening normative claims (23%), canonical quotes (9%), or theoretical positioning (5%). The world or the argument comes first.

### 8. The conclusion that ends with a summary

The dominant closing moves are forward-looking application (36%), calls to action (23%), and thesis crystallization (14%). Only one book (5%) closes with a scholarly contribution statement, and even that is forward-looking. Ending with "in this book, I have shown that..." without pivoting to what comes next is a missed opportunity.

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Cluster Selection & Architecture

**Goal:** Read the user's manuscript description, select the right cluster, and map the structural sequence before any drafting begins.

**Steps:**
1. Read the book project memo, introduction, and empirical chapter summaries
2. Confirm with the user: What is the core argument? What kind of evidence? What happened since the fieldwork period? What should the conclusion accomplish beyond summary?
3. Walk through the decision tree with the user
4. Select a primary cluster and identify any secondary cluster elements to borrow
5. Map the structural sequence with approximate word counts per section
6. Determine:
   - Opening type (thesis restatement, anecdote, normative claim, canonical quote)
   - New material to introduce (prescriptions, new cases, theoretical connections, updated data)
   - Scope shift type (general theory, democratic theory, temporal extension, comparative, methodological)
   - Closing move (forward-looking, call to action, thesis crystallization, diagnostic warning)
   - Target word count (use cluster benchmarks)

**Pause:** Confirm cluster selection and structural plan before drafting.

---

### Phase 2: Drafting

**Goal:** Draft each section of the conclusion following the selected cluster's sequence, applying word count targets.

**Steps:**
1. Read `techniques/section-recipes.md` for the recipe matching your selected cluster. Read `techniques/paragraph-functions.md` for paragraph-level moves.
2. Draft the opening. The first 1--3 paragraphs must accomplish the thesis restatement (100% frequency) and begin the callback to the introduction (100% frequency). Use the opening type prescribed by your cluster.
3. Draft the cross-case synthesis section. This is the conclusion's core analytical deliverable for most clusters. Consider whether a new comparative table, typology, or matrix belongs here (86% of conclusions include one).
4. Draft the new material sections -- prescriptions, new cases, theoretical connections, or updated data, as determined by your cluster.
5. Draft the scope shift passage. Every conclusion broadens beyond its cases; determine which type of broadening is appropriate.
6. Draft the forward-looking element (91% frequency).
7. Draft the closing move, matching the type prescribed by your cluster.
8. Verify all eight universal moves are present:
   - [ ] Thesis restated in first 1--3 paragraphs
   - [ ] Callback to introduction's framing device
   - [ ] Scope shift beyond empirical cases
   - [ ] New material introduced
   - [ ] Normative tone shift present
   - [ ] Forward-looking element included
   - [ ] Cross-case synthesis produced
   - [ ] Broader literature engaged

**Pause:** Review the full draft against the structural plan. Check word counts per section.

---

### Phase 3: Calibration & Revision

**Goal:** Test the draft against cluster benchmarks and the eight universal moves. Revise as needed.

**Calibration checks:**

| Test | Pass Criteria |
|------|---------------|
| **Total word count** | Within cluster range (see cluster benchmark tables) |
| **Conclusion-to-introduction ratio** | 50--70% of introduction length (median 1:1.7) |
| **Subsection count** | Within cluster range |
| **Citation density** | Matches cluster prescription (low, moderate, high) |
| **Opening type** | Matches cluster prescription |
| **Closing type** | Matches cluster prescription |

**Universal move tests:**

| Test | Question | Fix if Failing |
|------|----------|---------------|
| **Thesis test** | Can the reader identify the restated thesis in the first 1--3 paragraphs? | Add a clear restatement paragraph with signal phrases ("This book has argued that...") |
| **Callback test** | Does the conclusion explicitly return to the introduction's central question, framework, or opening device? | Add a passage that mirrors or responds to the introduction's framing |
| **Scope test** | Does the conclusion claim significance beyond the specific cases? | Add a broadening passage -- to general theory, democratic theory, temporal extension, or comparative implications |
| **New material test** | Is there at least one element not available in any prior chapter? | Add new material appropriate to your cluster: prescriptions, new cases, theoretical connections, or updated data |
| **Tone test** | Does the conclusion's voice differ from the empirical chapters toward normative/prescriptive? | Revise key passages to include evaluative language, "should" statements, or explicit value claims |
| **Forward test** | Does the conclusion look ahead? | Add a prospective passage -- future research, future politics, or conditional projections |
| **Synthesis test** | Is there a comparative insight that integrates across cases? | Add a cross-case comparison, table, matrix, or set of meta-level propositions |
| **Literature test** | Does the conclusion engage at least one scholarly conversation not central to the empirical chapters? | Add a passage connecting findings to an adjacent literature |

**Revision priorities** (in order):
1. Thesis restatement clarity -- if the reader cannot find the restated argument, the conclusion is disoriented
2. Callback to introduction -- if the conclusion does not create structural closure, the book feels incomplete
3. New material -- if nothing is new, the conclusion is merely a summary
4. Cross-case synthesis -- if the conclusion does not produce a comparative deliverable, it has not earned its place
5. Scope shift -- if the conclusion stays within the book's empirical boundaries, it has not done its broadening work
6. Forward-looking element -- if the conclusion is entirely retrospective, it ends without momentum
7. Word count -- trim or expand to match cluster benchmarks
