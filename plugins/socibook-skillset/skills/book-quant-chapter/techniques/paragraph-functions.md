# Paragraph Functions in Sequential Quantitative Evidence Chapters

This guide identifies eleven paragraph-level functions that recur across quantitative evidence chapters in academic monographs. Each function has a defined purpose, a characteristic internal structure, and real verbatim examples drawn from Grumbach (2023), Tesler (2016), Bolton & Thrower (2022), and Kadivar (2022).

Use this guide in two ways: (1) when drafting, to decide what each paragraph needs to accomplish; (2) when revising, to diagnose paragraphs that feel flat, redundant, or disconnected from the cumulative argument.

---

## The Eleven Paragraph Functions

### 1. BRIDGE_FROM_PRIOR

**Purpose:** Connect this chapter to the cumulative argument by restating what prior chapters established and identifying the question that remains. This is the paragraph that makes a book chapter a book chapter rather than a standalone article.

**Typical position:** Paragraph 1 or 2. In Domain Extender chapters, it appears in the first two sentences. In Payoff Chapters, it can occupy an entire opening section.

**How it works in practice:**

Grumbach (2023), Chapter 5, opens with the most efficient bridge in the corpus -- a single question that simultaneously restates the prior finding and states the new question:

> "If not ordinary voters, who might be driving state policy resurgence? As I argued in the last chapter, state politics might be advantageous for people and groups with more time, money, information, coordination, and mobility."

The first sentence restates the prior chapter's finding (ordinary voters are not the drivers) as a question. The second sentence cites "the last chapter" explicitly and names the theoretical prediction. Two sentences accomplish both the backward and forward connections.

Tesler (2016), Chapter 3, bridges through an intermediary -- Charlie Crist's own testimony -- before citing the prior chapter:

> "Governor Crist's statements raised two important questions about the effects of racial attitudes on mass assessments of public figures during Barack Obama's presidency... we know from the previous chapter that they remained an important cause of 2012 voter preferences."

The bridge is embedded in the anecdote. "We know from the previous chapter" is the explicit backward reference; the "two important questions" are the forward reference.

Bolton and Thrower (2022), Chapter 6, bridge by restating the mechanism established in two prior chapters:

> "As we demonstrate in chapters 4 and 5, Congress's command over the executive branch critically depends on its resource capacity. In this chapter and the next, we consider how capacity impacts the incentives of executives contemplating whether to go it alone."

This bridge names the specific prior chapters by number, restates their finding in a single sentence, and pivots with "In this chapter and the next" to announce the new question.

Kadivar (2022), Chapter 2, bridges from the theory chapter by restating the hypothesis and naming the contrasting cases:

> "My argument in the previous chapter suggests that the different outcomes of democratic transitions in South Africa and Pakistan might have something to do with the paths the two countries took to democratization."

**Internal structure:**

1. **Backward reference** -- what prior chapters showed, either through explicit citation ("as I argued in the last chapter") or through restatement of the finding.
2. **Gap or question** -- what remains unexplained or untested.
3. **This chapter's task** -- what this chapter will do to address the gap.

**Critical rule:** The bridge must be SPECIFIC about both what was shown and what remains. "The previous chapters have established the book's argument" is not a bridge -- it restates nothing the reader can hold onto. Grumbach's "If not ordinary voters, who?" is a bridge because it presupposes the prior finding (voters are not driving it) and states the question (then who is?).

**Diagnostic:** Can the reader state both what has been established and what question this chapter addresses, after reading this paragraph?

### 2. QUESTION_STATE

**Purpose:** Name the specific empirical question this chapter will answer. The research question is the hinge between the bridge and the analysis.

**Typical position:** Paragraph 2 or 3, immediately after the bridge. Sometimes fused with the bridge (as in Grumbach Ch. 5).

**How it works in practice:**

Kadivar (2022) states the question with maximum clarity after the South Africa/Pakistan contrast:

> "Notwithstanding that these two cases support this book's central proposition, what can we learn from the remaining countries that democratized during recent decades, of which we find 113 other democratic transitions between 1950 and 2010? Is there a meaningful association between unarmed mobilization leading to democratization and democratic durability, even when taking into account the extensive differences between these countries? Such an inquiry is the intent of this chapter."

Three questions in sequence, each more precise than the last. "Such an inquiry is the intent of this chapter" is the explicit closure.

Grumbach (2023), Chapter 3, embeds the question in a rhetorical opposition between politicians and political scientists:

> "So, are state-level politicians overstating their centrality to policymaking in the United States? Or is political science due for an update? In this chapter, I argue the latter."

The question is framed as a choice between two positions; the answer is given immediately. This is a common move in Domain Extender and Foundation Layer chapters: state the question, then preview the answer in the same paragraph.

Bolton and Thrower (2022), Chapter 4, state the question through a policy illustration:

> "How do Congress and other legislatures decide how much policymaking discretion to cede to executive actors?"

A single sentence, placed after the Trump travel ban illustration. The question is simultaneously specific (about discretion) and general (about separation of powers).

**Internal structure:**

1. **Context** -- the gap or puzzle established by the bridge.
2. **The question** -- stated as a direct interrogative or as a "this chapter investigates whether..." formulation.
3. **Preview** -- optionally, a compressed statement of the answer ("I argue the latter"; "I find that...").

**Critical rule:** The question must be ANSWERABLE with data. "Why is democracy important?" is not a research question. "Is there a meaningful association between unarmed mobilization and democratic durability, even when taking into account the extensive differences between these countries?" is a research question -- it specifies the relationship, the scope, and the condition for a satisfying answer.

**Diagnostic:** Can the reader state the question in one sentence after reading this paragraph?

### 3. DATA_INTRODUCE

**Purpose:** Establish the evidentiary basis for the chapter's analysis. This paragraph names the dataset, the key variables, and the analytical approach in enough detail to create trust without burying the reader in technical detail.

**Typical position:** After the research question, typically in a labeled "Data and Methods" or "Measures" section. In Foundation Layer chapters, this is a substantial section. In Domain Extender chapters, it may be a single paragraph.

**How it works in practice:**

Grumbach (2023), Chapter 3, introduces his dataset in a paragraph that also signals the chapter's two key empirical concepts:

> "The policy consequences of this shift have been profound. I use data on 135 major state policies to investigate two particular policy dynamics in the fifty states: increased policy variation (the substantive differences between states) and policy polarization (the relationship between party control and policy outcomes)."

One sentence names the dataset (135 policies), the unit (fifty states), and the two outcome concepts (variation and polarization). The parenthetical definitions tell the reader exactly what these terms mean.

Tesler (2016) introduces each chapter's data source with a "Thankfully, we can test that" pivot:

> "Thankfully, we can test that expectation with the 2011-12 Cooperative Campaign Analysis Project (CCAP). This unique election study interviewed hundreds of thousands of Americans in late 2011 and then reinterviewed fresh nationally representative subsamples (N = 1,000) from that baseline every week of the campaign."

"Thankfully" signals that a data source exists to answer the question just posed. The reader learns the study name, N, and the weekly reinterview design in two sentences.

Kadivar (2022) uses a formal methods paragraph:

> "In this chapter, I use three methods to analyze the effect of mass mobilization for democratic survival and democratic quality. For democratic survival, I rely on event history analysis, and for democratic and civil society quality, I adopt a random effects model. Finally, I use the negative binomial model to estimate the effect of different variables on the length of unarmed mobilization."

Three methods mapped to three outcomes. The reader knows exactly which analytical tool addresses which question.

Bolton and Thrower (2022) combine data and theoretical predictions in a single paragraph:

> "To test these theoretical arguments, we use a new measure of discretion based on the appropriations process from fiscal years 1960 to 2012, along with four indicators of resource capacity -- committee staff sizes, staffer experience, committee member experience, and chair experience."

The phrase "To test these theoretical arguments" connects the data to the theory. The reader learns the measure (appropriations-based), the time span (1960--2012), and the four capacity indicators.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Connection to the question** -- "To test this" or "To investigate whether."
2. **Dataset name, N, and time span** -- the reader needs these three facts.
3. **Key variables** -- independent and dependent, named and briefly defined.
4. **Analytical approach** -- the type of model or method, named.
5. **Optional pointer** -- "details of measurement are in the appendix" or "I describe the coding in the next section."

**Critical rule:** The DATA_INTRODUCE paragraph must create TRUST. The reader should believe the data can answer the question. Naming the N, the time period, and the measure of the key variable accomplishes this. Exhaustive coding documentation does not -- save it for the appendix.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader know what data, what measures, and what methods will be used?

### 4. TABLE_SETUP

**Purpose:** Tell the reader what to look for in the table or figure that follows. This paragraph primes the reader's attention so they can find the key result in the output.

**Typical position:** Immediately before a table or figure reference.

**How it works in practice:**

Tesler (2016) sets up his signature weekly-coefficient figure:

> "The first test of this chapter's main hypothesis that Barack Obama's presidency helped racialize white Americans' 2010 and 2012 voting behavior for Congress compares the impact of racial attitudes on Republican vote preference for the House of Representatives in 2010 and 2012 to their average effects on congressional vote choices in the pre-Obama era (1986-2004). Figure 6.1 graphically presents that over-time comparison."

The setup paragraph names: (1) the hypothesis being tested, (2) the comparison (Obama era vs. pre-Obama era), (3) the dependent variable (congressional vote preference), and (4) the figure number. When the reader looks at Figure 6.1, they know what to look for.

Kadivar (2022) sets up his descriptive table:

> "Keeping other variables at their observed values, the relative possibility of breakdown for a democracy with one year of unarmed mobilization is 18 percent less than a democracy that emerged without unarmed mobilization."

Here the setup paragraph actually delivers the result in text before the reader reaches the table, converting the coefficient into a percentage reduction. The table then serves as confirmation, not revelation.

Bolton and Thrower (2022) set up their interaction analysis:

> "When staff or experience is low, we find interbranch conflict is unrelated to congressional discretionary decisions. As committee resources increase, however, the ally principle obtains."

This gives the reader the two-part finding before the table: null effect at low capacity, significant effect at high capacity. The reader knows exactly which cells in the regression table matter.

**Internal structure:**

1. **What is being tested** -- the hypothesis or comparison.
2. **What to look for** -- the key column, row, coefficient, or trend in the table/figure.
3. **Optional preview** -- the finding stated in text before the table.

**Critical rule:** Never present a table or figure without a setup paragraph. "Table 3 presents the results" is not a setup -- it tells the reader nothing about what to look for. The setup must direct the reader's eyes to the result that matters.

**Diagnostic:** After reading this paragraph, does the reader know where to look in the table?

### 5. TABLE_INTERPRET

**Purpose:** State what the table or figure shows, in the author's own analytical voice, after the reader has seen the results. This paragraph converts statistical output into a substantive claim.

**Typical position:** Immediately after a table or figure.

**How it works in practice:**

Grumbach (2023) interprets the IGA effect with maximal substantive translation:

> "The results show that as the share of IGA fundraising for Republican candidates in a state grows, the Republicans in the state legislature become more conservative. The effect is larger than those of the legislator level analysis shown earlier and is massive in substantive terms: a one standard deviation increase in the percent of money that comes from IGA donors makes the state's average Republican legislator 0.33 standard deviations more conservative in office."

"Massive in substantive terms" -- this is the author interpreting, not just reporting. The coefficient is translated into standardized units with an explicit magnitude judgment.

Tesler (2016) interprets his pre/post comparison figure:

> "Those modest 1986-2004 effects nearly tripled in a pair of pooled 2010 preelection ANES surveys, though. The dashed line in the top-left panel of figure 6.1, for instance, shows that the same change from least to most racially resentful was now associated with a thirty-five-percentage-point increase in 2010 Republican vote preferences."

"Nearly tripled" is the interpretive judgment. "Thirty-five-percentage-point increase" is the quantitative translation.

Bolton and Thrower (2022) interpret their key finding about congressional capacity:

> "Overall, we provide critical evidence for the policy effects of capacity on executive discretion, a fundamental feature of separation of powers systems. While select studies show the relationship between capacity and discretion in other contexts like the US states (Huber and Shipan 2002), this chapter represents, to our knowledge, the first evidence that legislative capacity is a binding constraint on discretionary grants at the federal level."

"To our knowledge, the first evidence" -- this is a novelty claim embedded in the interpretation. The reader knows not just what the result is but what it means for the field.

Kadivar (2022) delivers interpretation through cumulative percentage reductions:

> "A democracy emerging from six years of mobilization has a relative risk of failure that is 46 percent less than a democracy with three years of mobilization, 64 percent less than a democracy with one year, and 71 percent less than a democracy in which unarmed mobilization did not occur."

Each comparison point adds to the persuasive force. The reader experiences the effect growing as the mobilization duration increases.

**Internal structure:**

1. **What the result shows** -- the direction and magnitude, stated plainly.
2. **Substantive translation** -- the coefficient rendered in real-world terms (percentage points, standard deviations, relative risk reductions).
3. **Significance for the argument** -- what this result means for the chapter's question or the book's cumulative case.

**Critical rule:** The TABLE_INTERPRET paragraph must contain a JUDGMENT. "The coefficient is 0.33" is reporting; "the effect is massive in substantive terms" is interpretation. The reader needs both: the number AND the analytical assessment of what the number means.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader know both the result and its significance after reading this paragraph?

### 6. FIGURE_NARRATE

**Purpose:** Walk the reader through a figure sequentially, directing their eyes across the visual display. Figures in quantitative chapters are not self-interpreting -- the author must narrate them.

**Typical position:** Immediately after presenting a figure, or fused with TABLE_INTERPRET when the result is a figure rather than a table.

**How it works in practice:**

Tesler (2016) narrates his weekly-coefficient plot by describing the two lines:

> "The solid line in the first panel of the display discloses a modest, but statistically significant (p < .01), relationship between racial resentment and support for Republican congressional candidates in the two decades before Obama became president. After controlling for party identification and ideological self-placement, moving from least to most racially resentful was associated with about a twelve-percentage-point increase in Republican vote share among white ANES respondents surveyed between 1986 and 2004."

First the solid line (pre-Obama baseline), then the magnitude (twelve percentage points), then the dashed line (Obama era) in the next paragraph. The reader's eyes follow the author's text across the figure.

Grumbach (2023) narrates his three-panel figure showing parallel trends:

The chapter presents a "three-panel figure showing trend in legislative polarization alongside trends in PAC share and IGA share, making the visual case that aggregate IGA growth (not PAC growth) tracks legislative polarization."

**Internal structure:**

1. **Name the visual element** -- "the solid line," "the top-left panel," "the dashed bars."
2. **State what it shows** -- direction, magnitude, pattern.
3. **Name the contrast** -- compare to another element in the same figure.
4. **State the implication** -- what the visual comparison reveals.

**Critical rule:** Narrate figures panel by panel, element by element. Never assume the reader will see what you see. If the figure has multiple panels, narrate them in order.

**Diagnostic:** Could a reader who cannot see the figure reconstruct its main message from the narration?

### 7. MECHANISM_CLAIM

**Purpose:** State that the results support the book's proposed mechanism. This paragraph connects the statistical finding to the theoretical argument, asserting that the results are not just a pattern but evidence of a specific causal process.

**Typical position:** After the main results have been presented and interpreted, before robustness checks. Most prominent in Mechanism Test chapters.

**How it works in practice:**

Grumbach (2023), Chapter 5, connects IGA effects to the book's nationalization thesis:

> "Organized groups are more than their staff members, their offices, and their formally incorporated nonprofit organizations. They are networks of activists and patrons. This investigation of the role of organizationally affiliated donors offers a more complete picture of the influence of groups on the polarization of state legislatures and the resurgence of state policy. It also provides an additional explanation for the way state policy changed dramatically in recent years without much change in public opinion."

The mechanism claim is: organizationally affiliated donors (not ordinary voters, not PACs) are the pathway through which national groups polarize state legislatures.

Bolton and Thrower (2022) make the mechanism claim by naming what the result overturns:

> "We reveal a ubiquitous prediction in the theoretical literature on discretion -- the ally principle -- is contingent on congressional capacity. Our findings are reproduced using several measures of resource capacity, with improvements to research design and measurement that give us confidence in the results. These patterns cast doubt on the prevailing wisdom in the separation of powers literature that Congress can be assumed to have sufficient resources to restrict the discretion of ideologically opposed executive actors."

"Cast doubt on the prevailing wisdom" is the mechanism claim: the ally principle is not unconditional; capacity is the binding constraint.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Restate the finding** -- briefly, in one sentence.
2. **Name the mechanism** -- what causal process does the finding reveal?
3. **Connect to the book's theory** -- how does this mechanism fit the cumulative argument?
4. **State what the mechanism explains** -- what outcome or puzzle does this resolve?

**Critical rule:** The mechanism claim must go beyond the statistical result. "The coefficient is significant" is a result; "interest group activists, not responsive public opinion, drive state-level policy change" is a mechanism claim. The mechanism claim is the paragraph that makes the result matter for the book.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader know not just what the result is, but what causal process it reveals?

### 8. ROBUSTNESS_TRANSITION

**Purpose:** Pivot from the main results to robustness checks and alternative explanations. This paragraph acknowledges the reader's likely skepticism and signals that the author has considered it.

**Typical position:** After the main results and mechanism claim, at the start of a robustness or alternative explanations section.

**How it works in practice:**

Grumbach (2023) transitions with an explicit acknowledgment of the endogeneity concern:

> "Donors and politicians act strategically in ways that make it difficult to study causal relationships in campaign finance research. It could be the case that politicians cause changes to donor behavior rather than the other way around (what social scientists call endogeneity). But while we should be cautious about interpreting these effects as causal, this investigation of individual affiliates of interest groups provides a partial explanation for partisan polarization."

The transition names the concern (endogeneity), defines it for a non-specialist reader ("what social scientists call endogeneity"), and signals the author's response ("while we should be cautious").

Bolton and Thrower (2022) use a "readers may be concerned" formulation:

> "As in the last chapter, readers may be concerned that capacity is caused by interbranch conflict. To address these endogeneity concerns, we again regress logged staff onto divided government, finding the effect is small and statistically insignificant."

The transition names the specific concern, references the same concern in the prior chapter ("as in the last chapter"), and delivers the robustness result in the same paragraph.

Bolton and Thrower also use a temporal-alternative transition:

> "Of course, other differences in US politics before and after 1945 might plausibly explain these results. To more directly examine the consequences of capacity, we analyze the entire executive order time series from 1905 through 2019."

"Of course" concedes the concern. "To more directly examine" signals the robustness strategy.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Name the concern** -- what might a skeptical reader worry about?
2. **Validate the concern** -- acknowledge it is legitimate ("of course," "readers may be concerned," "it could be the case").
3. **Signal the response** -- "to address this," "to more directly examine," "but while we should be cautious."

**Critical rule:** The transition must VALIDATE the concern before dismissing it. A robustness section that opens with "our results are robust" without first naming what they are robust TO reads as defensive rather than rigorous.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader feel that the author has anticipated their most likely objection?

### 9. ALTERNATIVE_ADDRESS

**Purpose:** Engage with a specific rival explanation and use data to demonstrate that it does not account for the findings. This paragraph goes beyond robustness (showing results survive alternative specifications) to directly confronting an alternative theoretical account.

**Typical position:** Within the robustness section, or integrated into the results presentation (as in Kadivar and Tesler).

**How it works in practice:**

Grumbach (2023) addresses the alternative that exceptions disprove the rule by reframing them as theoretically informative:

> "Unlike 14 of 16 policy areas as wide-ranging as environmental regulation, LGBT rights, and health policy -- Democratic and Republican states both implemented similar 'tough on crime' laws that led to mass incarceration and were equally unwilling or unable to rein in the racial authoritarianism of the police forces under their command. This exceptionalism of policing and criminal justice in defying the polarization pattern highlights a broader lack of attention by political scientists to the role of policing in modern racial authoritarianism."

The exception (criminal justice) is not buried but elevated into a finding of its own. The alternative ("partisan polarization does not explain criminal justice policy") is acknowledged and then reframed as evidence for a different argument about racial authoritarianism.

Grumbach (2023), Chapters 7--8, addresses the racial threat alternative:

> "Furthermore, while Republican control still has a large negative effect on democratic performance, the interaction of Republican control and demographic change matters little. Unexpectedly, the one statistically significant coefficient involving demographic change is the positive coefficient for the interaction of Republican control and Latino population growth."

The alternative (racial demographic threat drives backsliding) is tested directly with an interaction term and found null. "Unexpectedly" signals that this null finding is itself informative.

Tesler (2016) addresses alternatives through embedded experiments:

> "Neither the 2012 campaign nor experimental treatments that have a proven track record of changing racial attitudes' impact on mass assessments of public figures altered the negative relationship between racial resentment and support for President Obama."

The experiments constitute the alternative-address: if the resentment-Obama link were an artifact of campaign context or measurement timing, the experimental treatments would have changed it. They did not.

Kadivar (2022) addresses the alternative that mere occurrence (not duration) matters:

> "Model 4 uses a dummy for any mobilization (vs. zero) rather than continuous duration -- finding this dummy is null while continuous duration remains significant, directly supporting the duration argument over the mere occurrence argument."

The most elegant form of alternative-address: testing the rival's preferred operationalization and showing it fails.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Name the alternative** -- what rival explanation exists?
2. **Test it** -- what specification, interaction, or design tests the rival?
3. **Report the result** -- what happened when the rival was tested?
4. **State the implication** -- what does this mean for the book's preferred explanation?

**Critical rule:** The alternative must be a REAL rival, not a straw man. "Some might argue that the moon causes policy change" is a straw man. "It could be the case that politicians cause changes to donor behavior rather than the other way around" is a real rival because reverse causality is the standard concern in campaign finance research.

**Diagnostic:** Would a skeptical reviewer of this chapter agree that the most important rival explanation has been addressed?

### 10. VERDICT_STATE

**Purpose:** Deliver the chapter's main finding in quotable form. This is the paragraph a reviewer will cite to summarize what the chapter demonstrates.

**Typical position:** In a labeled "Conclusion" or "Summary" section, typically the first or second paragraph. Sometimes restated at the end of the chapter before the forward bridge.

**How it works in practice:**

Grumbach (2023), Chapters 7--8, delivers the book's most consequential verdict:

> "In the contemporary period, however, the results are overwhelmingly clear -- and concerning. The results of this analysis point to the Republican Party as the antidemocracy coalition in American politics and state governments as a key venue in which they are pursuing their goals. Pointing squarely at the GOP -- naming names -- is not common in American politics research or journalism."

"Overwhelmingly clear -- and concerning." "Pointing squarely at the GOP -- naming names." This is a verdict with normative weight. The phrase "naming names" is the author's metadiscursive move -- acknowledging that the finding is politically charged.

Kadivar (2022) delivers the verdict by contrasting it with the rival theory:

> "The elitist approach to democratization maintains that democracies that emerge from popular mobilization are more likely to collapse. Instead, I propose that democracies forged through sustained unarmed mobilization are more likely to survive and thrive... An analysis of the data confirms a robust and positive relationship between the length of unarmed mobilization and the chance of new democracies to survive."

The verdict is stated against the foil of the rival theory. "Instead" marks the pivot from what the rival claims to what the data show.

Bolton and Thrower (2022) deliver the verdict by naming the mechanism:

> "The effects of legislative capacity are not simply confined to the actions of Congress, but instead ripple throughout the separation of powers systems. Presidential views of unilateral policy opportunities are in part determined by legislative majorities' capacity to constrain their behavior."

"Ripple throughout" is the interpretive judgment; "determined by legislative majorities' capacity" is the mechanism restated as the conclusion.

Tesler (2016) delivers the synthesis verdict with the book's title phrase:

> "America in the Obama era was not post-racial. It was most-racial."

The shortest verdict in the corpus -- two sentences that restate the book's title as an empirical conclusion.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Signal phrase** -- "the results are clear," "I find that," "this chapter demonstrates."
2. **The claim** -- what the data show, stated directly.
3. **Significance** -- what this means for the book's argument or for the field.

**Critical rule:** The verdict must be QUOTABLE. If a reviewer or colleague cannot extract a 1--2 sentence summary of this chapter from the verdict paragraph, it has failed. Test: read the verdict paragraph in isolation. Does it communicate the chapter's central finding?

**Diagnostic:** Can you quote this paragraph to summarize what the chapter shows?

### 11. BRIDGE_FORWARD

**Purpose:** Connect to the next chapter by naming what it will do and explaining why the current chapter's findings make that next step necessary. The forward bridge closes the loop: this chapter answered question Y; question Z now arises.

**Typical position:** Final paragraph or final two paragraphs of the chapter.

**How it works in practice:**

Grumbach (2023), Chapter 3, bridges forward with the most explicit preview in the corpus:

> "In the next chapter, I describe how national activists and activist groups became more coordinated and invested in state politics in recent years. While state opinion has been mostly static, these activists have increased their participation in state politics -- and transformed state governments in the process."

Two sentences: what the next chapter will do, and why it matters. "While state opinion has been mostly static" presupposes the current chapter's finding (opinion is not the driver) and creates the puzzle the next chapter will address (if not opinion, then activists).

Kadivar (2022) bridges with a methodological pivot:

> "The findings of this chapter confirm a robust association between unarmed mobilization and democratic durability, but what are the mechanisms underlying this association? Why do we observe a higher chance for survival and improvement among democracies born out of sustained mobilization? I explore these questions in the following chapters."

"But what are the mechanisms" is the pivot. The chapter established the pattern; the next chapters will explain it.

Bolton and Thrower (2022) bridge by naming the limitation that the next chapter overcomes:

> "While these findings offer firm evidence in favor of our theory, the analysis is limited in notable ways. First, the attention to one particular (albeit important) time series -- executive orders at the federal level -- is not ideal... To resolve these issues, we leverage variation in another context: the separation of powers systems across the fifty US states."

The bridge names the limitation (one time series, one level of government) and announces the solution (states as additional context). The reader knows both what was missing and how the next chapter will fill the gap.

Tesler (2016) bridges by connecting the current finding to future chapters with specific predictions:

> "As we will see in later chapters, this spillover of racialization into mass assessments of public figures had electoral consequences. Chapter 6 shows that racial attitudes had a stronger influence on congressional elections during Barack Obama's presidency than they had previously."

The bridge names the next chapter's finding as a consequence of the current chapter's finding. The reader experiences the cumulative logic directly.

**Internal structure:**

1. **Restate this chapter's finding** -- briefly, in one sentence.
2. **Name the remaining question** -- what this chapter could not answer.
3. **Preview the next chapter** -- what it will do, what data or method it will use.
4. **State the connection** -- why this chapter's finding makes the next chapter's question necessary.

**Critical rule:** The forward bridge must be SPECIFIC about the next chapter. "More research is needed" is not a bridge. "In the next chapter, I describe how national activists and activist groups became more coordinated" is a bridge -- it names the topic, the actors, and the analytical move. Every forward bridge in the corpus names the next chapter's subject.

**Diagnostic:** Does the reader know what the next chapter will do and why this chapter's findings make it necessary?

## Paragraph Sequences by Cluster

The four chapter clusters deploy these paragraph functions in characteristic sequences.

### Domain Extender

> Opens with a domain-specific anecdote, bridges briefly from the prior chapter, then moves efficiently through data, results, and extension verdict.

```
[Anecdote/case introducing new domain] --> BRIDGE_FROM_PRIOR --> QUESTION_STATE -->
DATA_INTRODUCE --> TABLE_SETUP --> TABLE_INTERPRET --> TABLE_SETUP --> TABLE_INTERPRET -->
FIGURE_NARRATE --> MECHANISM_CLAIM --> ROBUSTNESS_TRANSITION --> ALTERNATIVE_ADDRESS -->
VERDICT_STATE --> BRIDGE_FORWARD
```

**Exemplar:** Tesler's spillover chapters repeat this sequence for each new domain: anecdote (Charlie Crist, the Tea Party, Colin Powell), bridge ("we know from the previous chapter"), question ("did racialization extend to X?"), data, results for X, verdict ("not limited to... also extends to..."), bridge to next domain.

### Foundation Layer

> Opens with contrasting cases, moves through an extensive data section, presents descriptive then inferential results, and bridges to qualitative chapters.

```
[Contrasting cases] --> QUESTION_STATE --> DATA_INTRODUCE --> DATA_INTRODUCE -->
TABLE_SETUP --> TABLE_INTERPRET --> TABLE_SETUP --> TABLE_INTERPRET -->
FIGURE_NARRATE --> ROBUSTNESS_TRANSITION --> ALTERNATIVE_ADDRESS -->
VERDICT_STATE --> BRIDGE_FORWARD [methodological pivot]
```

**Exemplar:** Kadivar opens with Mandela/Bhutto, asks "what about the remaining 113 cases?", devotes substantial space to measures and methods, then presents survival analysis results. The bridge to qualitative chapters is explicitly methodological.

### Mechanism Test

> Opens by restating the pattern, then derives the mechanism prediction, introduces the mechanism variable, and tests it.

```
BRIDGE_FROM_PRIOR --> QUESTION_STATE [mechanism question] -->
[Theoretical derivation of mechanism] --> DATA_INTRODUCE [new variable] -->
TABLE_SETUP --> TABLE_INTERPRET --> MECHANISM_CLAIM -->
TABLE_SETUP --> TABLE_INTERPRET --> ROBUSTNESS_TRANSITION -->
ALTERNATIVE_ADDRESS --> VERDICT_STATE --> BRIDGE_FORWARD
```

**Exemplar:** Grumbach Ch. 5 opens with "If not ordinary voters, who?", introduces IGAs as a new variable, presents legislator-level results, then state-party-level results, then primary/general election results. Each results block is followed by interpretation and mechanism claims.

### Payoff Chapter

> Opens with a cumulative bridge summarizing prior chapters, presents the consequential dependent variable, and delivers the book's central finding.

```
BRIDGE_FROM_PRIOR [full cumulative summary] --> QUESTION_STATE -->
[Stakes paragraph] --> DATA_INTRODUCE [new DV or measurement] -->
```
